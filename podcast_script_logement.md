# Podcast Script — "The Housing Portal – Help to Rent, Buy and Renovate in Luxembourg"

**Part of the Biergerpakt programme**
**Hosts:** Anna (woman) and Tom (man)
**Length:** about 13 minutes — spoken slowly, in simple English

---

**ANNA:** Hello, and welcome back! It's good to have you with us today.
**TOM:** Hello everyone!
**ANNA:** This episode is part of the Biergerpakt. That is a programme about living together in Luxembourg. The idea is simple... we want to help residents, and cross-border workers too, to find their way in everyday life.
**TOM:** And few subjects are as important, or as stressful, as today's... housing.
**ANNA:** Finding a place to live, paying the rent, dreaming of buying, renovating an old home.
**TOM:** It can feel overwhelming, and expensive. But there's more help out there than many people realise.
**ANNA:** And it's gathered in one place... the housing portal of the Ministry of Housing.
**TOM:** The website is logement dot public dot lu.
**ANNA:** In an earlier episode we looked at one part of it, the housing observatory, which tracks prices.
**TOM:** Today we look at the whole portal, and especially the help it offers ordinary people.
**ANNA:** It's organised around who you are, which is really helpful.
**TOM:** Yes. There's a section for tenants, one for owners, and one for communes and professionals.
**ANNA:** Let's start with tenants, because renting is where many people begin.
**TOM:** The portal helps you look for a home, and understand your rights as a tenant.
**ANNA:** It explains the rental contract, the lease, so you know what you're signing.
**TOM:** And crucially, it explains the financial help available to renters.
**ANNA:** For example, there's a rent subsidy for households on lower incomes, to help with the cost of renting.
**TOM:** Many people who'd qualify don't even know it exists. So this is worth checking.
**ANNA:** There's also social rental management, which helps bring more affordable homes onto the market.
**TOM:** Now let's talk about owners, and people who dream of buying.
**ANNA:** Buying a home is a huge step, and the State offers several forms of support.
**TOM:** There's help linked to buying or building your first home, to make it a little more reachable.
**ANNA:** And there's a well-known measure that reduces the registration costs when you buy a home to live in.
**TOM:** The portal explains who can get what, and how to apply.
**ANNA:** And it's not only about buying. It's also about improving the home you already have.
**TOM:** Yes. There's support for renovation, and especially for making homes more energy-efficient.
**ANNA:** That connects to Klima-Agence, from another episode... they advise, and the housing aids help pay.
**TOM:** So the pieces fit together... advice on one side, financial help on the other.
**ANNA:** There's a very practical detail worth knowing... a single counter for housing aids.
**TOM:** A one-stop office where you can ask about, and apply for, the different housing supports.
**ANNA:** So you don't have to run between many offices. There's one door to knock on.
**TOM:** Let's talk about the bigger picture, too, because housing is a national priority.
**ANNA:** The portal explains the policy on affordable housing... building more homes that ordinary people can actually afford.
**TOM:** And the partnership with communes, so that towns and villages help create affordable homes locally.
**ANNA:** It's a long road, but the direction is clear... more, and more affordable, housing.
**TOM:** Let's make it really practical. What should someone do with all this?
**ANNA:** First, if you rent and your income is modest, check whether you qualify for the rent subsidy.
**TOM:** Second, if you're hoping to buy or build, look at the State's support before you start.
**ANNA:** Third, if you want to renovate, especially for energy, combine Klima-Agence advice with housing aids.
**TOM:** And fourth, when in doubt, contact the single counter for housing aids, and just ask.
**ANNA:** Let's bring it together. What should people remember about the housing portal?
**TOM:** First... logement dot public dot lu is the Ministry of Housing's one-stop portal, for tenants, owners and communes.
**ANNA:** Second... it explains tenants' rights and rental help, like the rent subsidy.
**TOM:** Third... it sets out support for buying, building and renovating a home.
**ANNA:** And fourth... there's a single counter for housing aids, and a national push for affordable housing.
**TOM:** A home is more than four walls. It's security, dignity, and a place to build a life.
**ANNA:** And knowing what help exists can bring that a little closer for many families.
**TOM:** Thank you so much for listening today.
**ANNA:** Take care, and see you in the next episode. Äddi!
**TOM:** Äddi!

---

*Sources: Logement.lu, logement.public.lu, of the Ministère du Logement et de l'Aménagement du territoire — the housing portal organised for tenants, owners and communes/professionals: finding a home and tenants' rights, rental aids (including the rent subsidy / subvention de loyer), the lease, social rental management; support for buying, building and renovating (including energy-efficient renovation) and the reduced registration costs for a main residence; the single counter for housing aids (Guichet unique des aides au logement); and the affordable-housing policy (LOGABO, Pacte Logement) with the communes. The housing observatory (Observatoire de l'Habitat) is covered in a separate episode. General information only — eligibility and amounts change; confirm current details with the Ministry. Accurate as of June 2026.*
