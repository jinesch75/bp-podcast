# Podcast Script — "The Luxembourg Space Agency – A Small Country Reaching for the Stars"

**Part of the Biergerpakt programme**
**Hosts:** Anna (woman) and Tom (man)
**Length:** about 13 minutes — spoken slowly, in simple English

---

**ANNA:** Hello, and welcome back! It's wonderful to have you with us today.
**TOM:** Hello everyone!
**ANNA:** This episode is part of the Biergerpakt. That is a programme about living together in Luxembourg. The idea is simple... we want to help residents, and cross-border workers too, to discover the country.
**TOM:** And today's topic might surprise you. It's about Luxembourg... and space.
**ANNA:** Yes, space! Satellites, rockets, the stars. It sounds like something for big countries only.
**TOM:** And yet, little Luxembourg is genuinely one of the world's space pioneers.
**ANNA:** To guide this, there's the Luxembourg Space Agency.
**TOM:** So Anna, how did a small country end up in space?
**ANNA:** The story starts back in 1985, when Luxembourg helped create a satellite company called SES.
**TOM:** SES became a world leader in beaming television and communications from space.
**ANNA:** From that seed, a whole space sector has grown here over the decades.
**TOM:** And the numbers are striking. In just over ten years, the sector has grown enormously.
**ANNA:** From a handful of companies and a few hundred people, to dozens of companies and well over a thousand jobs.
**TOM:** That growth came from strong government support, and a pioneering legal framework.
**ANNA:** Luxembourg was one of the first countries in Europe to make clear laws about activities in space.
**TOM:** So Anna, what does the Space Agency actually do?
**ANNA:** It develops and supports Luxembourg's space sector... helping companies, funding, and talent.
**TOM:** It works to attract space businesses to set up here, and to help local ones grow.
**ANNA:** And it connects Luxembourg to the big players, like the European Space Agency.
**TOM:** Now, you might be thinking... that's nice, but what has space got to do with my daily life?
**ANNA:** Far more than you'd imagine! Space quietly powers things we use every single day.
**TOM:** Think of the satellite navigation that guides your car, your bus, your delivery.
**ANNA:** Think of weather forecasts, and the television and communications that reach remote places.
**TOM:** Satellites also watch over the Earth... tracking the climate, pollution, forests, and floods.
**ANNA:** So space technology helps protect the environment, and respond to disasters.
**TOM:** It even helps farmers, by monitoring crops from above, as we mentioned in our agriculture episode.
**ANNA:** And research in space can advance medicine and new materials back here on Earth.
**TOM:** So space isn't escapism. It's a practical tool that improves life on the ground.
**ANNA:** There's also a thoughtful side to Luxembourg's space strategy... sustainability.
**TOM:** Yes. It's not only about going to space, but doing it responsibly.
**ANNA:** That means keeping space usable for future generations, and using space to solve challenges on Earth.
**TOM:** It's a mature way to think about a powerful new frontier.
**ANNA:** Let's talk about why this matters for ordinary residents, beyond the wonder of it.
**TOM:** First, jobs. The space sector creates skilled, future-facing jobs right here.
**ANNA:** Second, the economy. It diversifies the country, so it doesn't depend on just one industry.
**TOM:** Third, services. The technologies improve navigation, communications, weather and climate monitoring.
**ANNA:** And fourth, inspiration. It shows young people that, from Luxembourg, you really can reach for the stars.
**TOM:** That last one matters. A child here can dream of a career in space, and find it on their doorstep.
**ANNA:** Let's make it practical. What can people take from this?
**TOM:** First, know that Luxembourg is a real hub for the space industry, supported by its Space Agency.
**ANNA:** Second, remember that space technology powers everyday things, from navigation to climate monitoring.
**TOM:** Third, if you, or your children, love science and technology, this is a growing world with opportunities here.
**ANNA:** Let's bring it together. What should people remember about the Luxembourg Space Agency?
**TOM:** First... it develops and supports Luxembourg's space sector, which has grown remarkably since the 1980s.
**ANNA:** Second... that growth came from government support and pioneering space laws.
**TOM:** Third... space quietly powers daily life, from navigation and communications to climate and disaster monitoring.
**ANNA:** And fourth... Luxembourg's strategy stresses doing space sustainably and responsibly.
**TOM:** It's a beautiful thought. A tiny country, looking up, and helping shape humanity's future among the stars.
**ANNA:** And bringing real jobs, services and inspiration back down to Earth.
**TOM:** Thank you so much for listening today.
**ANNA:** Take care, keep looking up, and see you in the next episode. Äddi!
**TOM:** Äddi!

*Sources: Luxembourg Space Agency (LSA), space-agency.public.lu — Luxembourg's growth as a space hub since entering the sector in 1985 (with the creation of SES), supported by strong government funding and a pioneering legal framework; the agency develops the national space sector, attracts and supports companies, funding and talent, and works with partners such as the European Space Agency; the national space strategy emphasises sustainability. Sector figures (growth from about 16 companies and ~600 people in 2012 to around 80 companies and ~1,650 people by 2024) are from the LSA and are dated accordingly; promotional claims are attributed to the agency. General information only. Accurate as of June 2026.*
