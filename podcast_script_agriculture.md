# Podcast Script — "The Agriculture Portal – Food, Farming and Wine in Luxembourg"

**Part of the Biergerpakt programme**
**Hosts:** Anna (woman) and Tom (man)
**Length:** about 14 minutes — spoken slowly, in simple English

---

**ANNA:** Hello, and welcome back! It's lovely to have you with us today.
**TOM:** Hello everyone!
**ANNA:** This episode is part of the Biergerpakt. That is a programme about living together in Luxembourg. The idea is simple... we want to help residents, and cross-border workers too, to feel connected to the country.
**TOM:** And today's topic connects us all, three times a day... food.
**ANNA:** The bread on your table, the vegetables on your plate, the glass of wine on a Friday evening.
**TOM:** Behind all of that is farming, and the people who care for the land and the animals.
**ANNA:** And there is an official place that brings all of this together... the agriculture portal.
**TOM:** The website is agriculture dot public dot lu.
**ANNA:** It is run by the Ministry of Agriculture, Food and Viticulture.
**TOM:** "Viticulture" is a lovely word. It simply means the growing of grapes, for wine.
**ANNA:** So this portal covers three big things... farming, food, and wine.
**TOM:** So Anna, who actually does the work behind this ministry?
**ANNA:** The ministry oversees several specialised administrations, each with its own role.
**TOM:** One of the most important is known by its initials, ASTA.
**ANNA:** ASTA is the technical agricultural services administration. Think of it as the science and support behind farming.
**TOM:** It gives farmers advice, watches the weather, and warns about crop diseases and pests.
**ANNA:** It also supports more sustainable farming, with less chemical spraying and better use of natural fertiliser.
**TOM:** There is also a dedicated wine institute, based in Remich, on the Moselle river.
**ANNA:** Luxembourg has a real wine tradition along the Moselle... white wines, and the sparkling wine called Crémant.
**TOM:** The wine institute supports the winegrowers, and helps keep the quality high.
**ANNA:** And there is an administration that looks after animal health and welfare, and the safety of our food.
**TOM:** So from the field, to the animal, to the plate... each step has people watching over it.
**ANNA:** Let's talk about a theme that's growing fast... organic farming.
**TOM:** Organic farming works with nature, in a closed cycle, without synthetic chemicals.
**ANNA:** Luxembourg has a plan, called PAN-Bio, to grow the share of organic farming in the country.
**TOM:** So when you see organic products in the shop, there is a national effort behind that choice.
**ANNA:** Now let's talk about something close to many hearts... animal welfare.
**TOM:** Luxembourg has an animal protection law that is quite advanced.
**ANNA:** It protects the dignity, the safety and the wellbeing of animals, throughout their whole life.
**TOM:** And that's not only about farm animals. It matters for pet owners too.
**ANNA:** If you have a dog, a cat, or any animal, you have a duty to care for it properly. The portal has guidance on this.
**TOM:** Let's come back to wine for a moment, because it's part of Luxembourg's culture.
**ANNA:** The Moselle valley, in the east, is full of vineyards on sunny slopes.
**TOM:** And in summer, there are vineyard walks and activities, where you can learn how wine is made.
**ANNA:** The portal even lists events... like nature and vineyard trails, open to everyone.
**TOM:** So this isn't only for farmers. It's an invitation to discover where our food and drink come from.
**ANNA:** Let's talk about local and seasonal food, which is good for everyone.
**TOM:** Buying local, seasonal products supports the farmers near you, and often tastes better and travels less.
**ANNA:** The ministry encourages this link between the people who produce food and the people who eat it.
**TOM:** A short distance from field to fork is good for the land, the economy, and the community.
**ANNA:** Now, farming is not easy, and farmers don't do it alone.
**TOM:** They receive support, including aids under the European Union's Common Agricultural Policy.
**ANNA:** We talked about European funds in another episode. Farming is one of the big areas Europe supports.
**TOM:** And the ministry offers advice and training, to help farms be modern, sustainable, and resilient.
**ANNA:** Let's step back and ask... why does all this matter for someone who never sets foot on a farm?
**TOM:** First, because everyone eats. Good farming means safe, healthy, quality food on your plate.
**ANNA:** Second, because farmers shape the landscape... the fields, hedges and meadows we all enjoy.
**TOM:** Third, because animal welfare reflects the kind of society we want to be.
**ANNA:** And fourth, because supporting local food keeps the countryside alive, and the community strong.
**TOM:** So Tom, let's make it practical. What can someone do with all this?
**ANNA:** First, when you shop, look for local and seasonal products, and try organic ones.
**TOM:** Second, if you keep animals, check the portal's guidance to care for them well and within the law.
**ANNA:** Third, in summer, visit the Moselle, take a vineyard walk, and taste a local wine or Crémant.
**TOM:** And fourth, if you're curious about farming, food or wine, the portal has reliable information.
**ANNA:** Let's bring it all together. What should people remember?
**TOM:** First... the agriculture portal, agriculture dot public dot lu, covers farming, food and wine, from the Ministry of Agriculture, Food and Viticulture.
**ANNA:** Second... specialised bodies support it... ASTA for technical services, the wine institute in Remich, and a body for animal welfare and food safety.
**TOM:** Third... Luxembourg is growing organic farming and protects animal welfare by law.
**ANNA:** And fourth... by choosing local and seasonal food, we all play a part in a healthy food system.
**TOM:** Food connects every one of us to the land, and to each other.
**ANNA:** And knowing the story behind our plate helps us value it, and the people who make it possible.
**TOM:** Thank you so much for listening today.
**ANNA:** Take care, eat well, and see you in the next episode. Äddi!
**TOM:** Äddi!

*Sources: Agriculture portal, agriculture.public.lu, and the Ministère de l'Agriculture, de l'Alimentation et de la Viticulture (ma.gouvernement.lu) — information on agriculture, viticulture, food and agricultural products; the ministry oversees several specialised administrations, including the Administration des services techniques de l'agriculture (ASTA), the Institut viti-vinicole (wine institute) in Remich, and the body responsible for animal health and welfare and food safety. References include the PAN-Bio organic-farming action plan and Luxembourg's animal protection law (which protects the dignity, safety and wellbeing of animals); farmers are supported notably through the EU Common Agricultural Policy.
