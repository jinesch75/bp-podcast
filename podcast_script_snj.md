# Podcast Script — "The Service National de la Jeunesse – Empowering Young People in Luxembourg"

**Part of the Biergerpakt programme**
**Hosts:** Anna (woman) and Tom (man)
**Length:** about 15 minutes — spoken slowly, in simple English

---

**ANNA:** Hello, and welcome back! It's lovely to have you with us today.
**TOM:** Hello everyone!
**ANNA:** This episode is part of the Biergerpakt. That is a programme about living together in Luxembourg. The idea is simple... we want to help residents, and cross-border workers too, to understand the country and feel at home here.
**TOM:** And today we are talking about young people, and the organisation that exists to support them.
**ANNA:** It is called the Service National de la Jeunesse. In English, the National Youth Service.
**TOM:** People often shorten it to its initials... S, N, J.
**ANNA:** The SNJ has a lovely motto in Luxembourgish... "Jonker staark maachen". It means "empowering youth".
**TOM:** So Anna, what exactly is the SNJ?
**ANNA:** It is a public service of the State, created back in 1964. So it has been supporting young people for sixty years.
**TOM:** And it works under the Ministry of Education, Children and Youth.
**ANNA:** Its big mission is to help create an environment where young people can develop in a positive way.
**TOM:** To help them grow into responsible, active, and caring citizens.
**ANNA:** And it supports them through learning experiences, based on what is called non-formal education.
**TOM:** Non-formal education. That's an important idea. What does it mean, Anna?
**ANNA:** It means learning that happens outside the normal classroom... through activities, projects, and real experiences.
**TOM:** Learning by doing, by meeting others, by trying things out. Not exams and grades.
**ANNA:** Exactly. And participation is voluntary. Young people take part because they want to.
**TOM:** Let's look at what the SNJ actually offers. I think it helps to see it in a few parts.
**ANNA:** Good idea. The first part is supporting the whole youth sector.
**TOM:** The youth sector means youth centres, youth services, and youth organisations across the country.
**ANNA:** The SNJ helps municipalities and these organisations with advice, with funding, and by connecting them together.
**TOM:** So behind many local youth clubs and activities, there is quiet support from the SNJ.
**ANNA:** The second part is what they call experiences for youth.
**TOM:** This is where it gets fun. The SNJ runs educational centres with themed activity programmes.
**ANNA:** They group them under the word "Erliewen", which means "to experience".
**TOM:** For example, experiencing nature, at a centre in Hollenfels.
**ANNA:** Or experiencing sport, at the centre in Lultzhausen.
**TOM:** There is experiencing media, where young people learn how videos are made, and how to spot manipulation online.
**ANNA:** There is experiencing creativity, in a makerspace with new technologies.
**TOM:** There is experiencing teamwork, which builds cooperation and values for living together.
**ANNA:** And experiencing human rights, at Cinqfontaines, a place of remembrance about the Holocaust.
**TOM:** That one is powerful. It helps young people stand against antisemitism and racism.
**ANNA:** There is even experiencing languages... Luxembourgish, German, French, and English, in a fun, confidence-building way.
**TOM:** So a young person can grow, have fun, and learn, all at the same time.
**ANNA:** The third part is support for young people moving into working life.
**TOM:** This is really important, and it connects to other episodes we have done.
**ANNA:** Yes. The SNJ helps young people who are at risk of dropping out of school, or who have left school early.
**TOM:** And young people who have been inactive for a long time, not in school and not in work.
**ANNA:** For them, the SNJ offers a personal, caring approach. They help each young person build what they call a "life project".
**TOM:** A life project... a plan for the next steps, based on that person's own needs and abilities.
**ANNA:** They offer workshops, training, and practical alternatives to just sitting at home.
**TOM:** One of the most special is the voluntary service. Tell us about that, Anna.
**ANNA:** The voluntary service lets a young person commit to a real project of general interest, for a period of time.
**TOM:** It is true learning by doing. You gain experience, you discover what you enjoy, and you build confidence.
**ANNA:** It can be a stepping stone... towards training, towards a job, towards finding your direction.
**TOM:** You can find these opportunities on websites like volontaires dot lu.
**ANNA:** There are also discovery internships in companies, to get a real taste of the working world.
**TOM:** The SNJ also runs something called the Au Pair service.
**ANNA:** Yes. If a young person comes to Luxembourg as an au pair, living with a host family, the SNJ supervises that.
**TOM:** They make sure the law is respected, prepare the au pairs, and help if there is any conflict.
**ANNA:** So both the young person and the family are protected. You can learn more at au dash pair dot lu.
**TOM:** There is also a fourth part... listening to young people.
**ANNA:** The SNJ studies the situation of young people, working with the University of Luxembourg.
**TOM:** They run surveys, and publish a big national report on youth every five years.
**ANNA:** They also listen directly, through the youth council, the "Jugendrot", where young people speak up on public matters.
**TOM:** So young people are not just helped... they are heard.
**ANNA:** And there is BEE SECURE, which many people know.
**TOM:** BEE SECURE promotes the safe and responsible use of the internet and new technologies.
**ANNA:** Tips to stay safe online, to avoid risks, and to use technology wisely.
**TOM:** Let's give a sense of the scale of all this.
**ANNA:** The SNJ has five educational centres, and local youth antennas across the country for individual support.
**TOM:** And every year, tens of thousands of young people take part in its activities.
**ANNA:** So Tom, let's make this practical. How can a young person, or a parent, actually use the SNJ?
**TOM:** First... if you know a young person who is unsure what to do after school, the SNJ can help them build a life project.
**ANNA:** Second... if a young person wants real experience, look into the voluntary service.
**TOM:** Third... for fun, growth, and meeting others, explore the SNJ's activity centres and programmes.
**ANNA:** Fourth... for safety online, BEE SECURE has practical advice for young people and parents alike.
**TOM:** Let's bring it all together. What should people remember about the SNJ?
**ANNA:** First... it is Luxembourg's National Youth Service, here to empower young people, with the motto "Jonker staark maachen".
**TOM:** Second... it supports the whole youth sector, and runs rich, hands-on learning experiences.
**ANNA:** Third... it helps young people move into working life, including through the voluntary service.
**TOM:** And fourth... it listens to young people, and promotes safe use of technology through BEE SECURE.
**ANNA:** Growing up is a journey, with ups and downs. No young person should have to walk it alone.
**TOM:** And in Luxembourg, they don't have to. The SNJ is walking beside them.
**ANNA:** Thank you so much for listening today.
**TOM:** Take care, and see you in the next episode. Äddi!
**ANNA:** Äddi!
