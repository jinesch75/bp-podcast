# Podcast Script — "Eltereforum: Strong Parents, Strong Children"

**Part of the Biergerpakt programme**
**Hosts:** Anna (woman) and Tom (man)
**Length:** about 10 minutes — spoken slowly, in simple English

---

**ANNA:** Hello, and welcome! It's really nice to have you with us today.

**TOM:** Hello everyone!

**ANNA:** This episode is part of the Biergerpakt. That's a programme of living together in Luxembourg. The idea is simple... we want to help residents, and also cross-border workers, to discover Luxembourg — or re-discover it — and to meet new people.

**TOM:** Exactly. And today's topic is for anyone who has children, or who is about to have children. It's a service called the Eltereforum.

**ANNA:** Eltereforum. Okay, Tom — that's a Luxembourgish word. Let's start there. What does it mean?

**TOM:** So... "Elteren" means "parents", and "Forum" means a place to meet and talk. So Eltereforum is, literally, a "parents' forum". In English you could say a "parent forum".

**ANNA:** A parents' forum. So it's a place for parents.

**TOM:** Exactly. The official description is very warm. The parent forums are welcoming places for discussion, information and support — for all parents.

**ANNA:** For all parents. So not only for parents with problems?

**TOM:** No, no — and this is important. It's for everyone. The Eltereforum is generalist. It's a normal, friendly place where any parent can come, ask questions, learn something, and meet other parents. You don't need a special reason. You don't need to have a problem.

**ANNA:** Good. Because sometimes people think, "I'll ask for help only if something is wrong."

**TOM:** Right. But raising a child is a big job for everybody. So the idea is to support parents from the very beginning, and along the whole way.

**ANNA:** From the very beginning — what does that mean exactly?

**TOM:** It means from the moment you start your "parental project" — so, even before the baby is born, when you are planning or expecting — all the way through every stage of the child's life, until they become an adult.

**ANNA:** Wow. So from pregnancy until the child is eighteen.

**TOM:** Yes. The whole journey. Every age has its questions. A baby who doesn't sleep. A small child and screens. School. Teenagers. The Eltereforum is there for all of it.

**ANNA:** That's reassuring. Now tell me — who is behind this? Is it official?

**TOM:** It is fully official. The Eltereforum is coordinated by the Ministry of Education, Children and Youth. So it's a public service of the Luxembourg State. And there's a nice slogan behind it — "Strong parents, strong children". The idea being: when parents feel supported and confident, the children do better too.

**ANNA:** Strong parents, strong children. I like that. Okay, so practically — what actually happens at an Eltereforum? What can I do there?

**TOM:** Lots of things. Let me list the main ones. There are parent meetings — so, moments to get together and exchange with other parents. There are parenting classes — like little courses on raising children. There are activities for parents together with their children. And there are themed evenings, talks and lectures — for example, an evening about sleep, or about screens, or about reading.

**ANNA:** So courses, evenings, meetings, and parent-child activities.

**TOM:** Exactly. And they also run information campaigns. The whole thing is built so that you get good-quality information, and at the same time a place to meet other parents and professionals.

**ANNA:** That second part is lovely — meeting other parents. Because parenting can feel lonely sometimes.

**TOM:** It really can. And here you realise — okay, other parents have the same questions as me. I'm not alone. That alone helps a lot.

**ANNA:** Now, the question many will have — does it cost money?

**TOM:** Good question. The support is, in very large part, free. Most of what the Eltereforum offers is free of charge, and it's adapted to your situation in life.

**ANNA:** Mostly free. That's great. And the professionals you mentioned — what if I have a serious, specific problem? Can they handle everything?

**TOM:** That's an important point. The Eltereforum is generalist — it does not give specialised treatment. It's not a clinic or a therapy centre. But if you need something more specific, the team listens to you and guides you. They point you to the right specialised service. So it's also a door — a first place to go, that sends you in the right direction.

**ANNA:** So even if they can't solve everything, they help me find who can.

**TOM:** Exactly. A friendly first step.

**ANNA:** Okay, Tom — where are these forums? Are they all in the capital?

**TOM:** No, and that's the nice part. They are regional. They're being opened all across the country, step by step. The first one opened in Niederanven, in February 2023. Then came Bettembourg, Marnach, Bertrange, Esch-Belval, Ettelbruck, Hesperange, Lorentzweiler, Wiltz... and the tenth one opened in Differdange, in June 2026.

**ANNA:** Ten forums already, across the regions. So there's probably one not too far from home.

**TOM:** That's the goal — that every region has one nearby. Each regional forum works together with the local players — the municipalities, the schools, the day-care centres, the local associations. So it's really rooted in the community.

**ANNA:** And for parents who can't easily travel to a forum?

**TOM:** Ah — for that, there's something really charming. It's called the Elteremobil.

**ANNA:** The Elteremobil? Let me guess... "Eltere" for parents, and "mobil"...

**TOM:** *(laughs)* Exactly. It's a little mini-bus that travels around the country to go and meet parents, directly, where they are. So the Eltereforum doesn't only wait for you to come — sometimes it comes to you.

**ANNA:** That's wonderful. A parents' forum on wheels.

**TOM:** A parents' forum on wheels. I love that.

**ANNA:** Okay. And there's also a website, right?

**TOM:** Yes. The website is eltereforum dot l-u. That's e-l-t-e-r-e-f-o-r-u-m dot l-u. It's an information platform in itself. Roughly every two months, it gives parents information on educational topics.

**ANNA:** What kind of topics?

**TOM:** Everyday ones, very concrete. For example — children and media, so screens and phones. Reading. Play and movement. How to organise your child's free time and leisure. The practical questions every parent has.

**ANNA:** So even from home, on the website, I already get something useful.

**TOM:** Yes. And on the website you also find the agenda — the calendar of all the activities, evenings and courses at the different forums. So you can see what's happening near you, and when.

**ANNA:** Perfect. So let's make it really practical. If I'm a parent listening right now, and I want to get started — what do I do?

**TOM:** Very simple. First, go to the website, eltereforum.lu. Look at the agenda, and find the forum closest to you. Then just look at what's on offer — a talk, a course, a parent-child activity — and come along. You don't need a big reason. You're a parent, and that's enough.

**ANNA:** And it doesn't matter what age my child is?

**TOM:** Doesn't matter at all. Expecting a baby, a toddler, a school child, a teenager — there's something for every stage. And remember, it's for all parents, mostly free, and there's no problem too small to bring.

**ANNA:** That's the message I'd love people to take away. You don't have to wait for things to go wrong. You can just go, to learn, and to meet other parents.

**TOM:** Exactly. Think of it as a friendly place that's on your side. Strong parents, strong children.

**ANNA:** Okay, Tom — short summary to finish?

**TOM:** Sure. The Eltereforum is a network of parent forums in Luxembourg, coordinated by the Ministry of Education, Children and Youth. They're welcoming places for information, exchange and support — for all parents, from the very start of parenthood until the child is grown. They offer meetings, courses, talks and parent-child activities, mostly for free.

**ANNA:** And how to find it?

**TOM:** Go to eltereforum.lu, check the agenda, and find your nearest forum — there are now ten across the country. And if you can't come to them, the Elteremobil may come to you.

**ANNA:** Beautiful. So... if you're a parent, you are not alone. There's a whole network here to support you.

**TOM:** Really. Just go once. You'll feel it.

**ANNA:** That was our episode about the Eltereforum, part of the Biergerpakt — living together in Luxembourg, discovering the country, and meeting new people. Thank you so much for listening.

**TOM:** Thank you, everyone. Take care... and see you next time!

**ANNA:** Bye bye!
