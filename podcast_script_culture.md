# Podcast Script — "The Culture Portal – Discovering Cultural Life in Luxembourg"

**Part of the Biergerpakt programme**
**Hosts:** Anna (woman) and Tom (man)
**Length:** about 13 minutes — spoken slowly, in simple English

---

**ANNA:** Hello, and welcome back! It's wonderful to have you with us today.
**TOM:** Hello everyone!
**ANNA:** This episode is part of the Biergerpakt. That is a programme about living together in Luxembourg. The idea is simple... we want to help residents, and cross-border workers too, to feel part of the country's life.
**TOM:** And few things bring people together like today's topic... culture.
**ANNA:** Concerts, theatre, museums, festivals, exhibitions, dance, films, heritage.
**TOM:** For a small country, Luxembourg has an astonishingly rich cultural life.
**ANNA:** But here's the challenge. With so much happening, how do you find out what's on?
**TOM:** That's exactly where today's tool comes in... the culture portal, at culture dot lu.
**ANNA:** So Tom, what is the culture portal?
**TOM:** It's a website that gathers the essential information about cultural life in Luxembourg, in one place.
**ANNA:** It's the cultural sector's shop window, supported by the Ministry of Culture.
**TOM:** So instead of searching many different websites, you have one welcoming doorway.
**ANNA:** Let's talk about the heart of it... the agenda, the what's-on listing.
**TOM:** The agenda brings together cultural events from across the country.
**ANNA:** Concerts, plays, exhibitions, readings, festivals, family activities... all in one calendar.
**TOM:** So on a free evening, or a rainy Sunday, you can simply look and find something to do.
**ANNA:** And it's for the whole country, not just the capital.
**TOM:** That really matters. Culture happens in towns and villages everywhere, not only in one place.
**ANNA:** The portal also points you to the many cultural venues and institutions.
**TOM:** Museums, theatres, concert halls, cultural centres, galleries... places to discover.
**ANNA:** Luxembourg has wonderful museums and venues, and this helps you find the ones near you.
**TOM:** Let's talk about heritage, too, because culture isn't only new things.
**ANNA:** Yes. It's also castles, old towns, traditions, and the stories of the country.
**TOM:** There are special events like museum nights and heritage days, when doors open to everyone.
**ANNA:** Those are magical occasions to explore places you might never otherwise visit.
**TOM:** The portal isn't only for audiences, though. It's also for the people who make culture.
**ANNA:** That's a good point. It supports the cultural sector itself... the artists, the institutions, the organisers.
**TOM:** It's part of a bigger effort to develop culture in Luxembourg, with a long-term cultural plan.
**ANNA:** And to make culture more accessible, more inclusive, and more present in every region.
**TOM:** There's even cooperation with the communes, so that local culture is supported close to home.
**ANNA:** Let's talk about why culture matters so much, beyond simply being enjoyable.
**TOM:** First, because it brings people together... across languages, ages and backgrounds.
**ANNA:** In a country as international as Luxembourg, shared cultural moments help us feel like one community.
**TOM:** Second, because culture helps us understand each other, and ourselves.
**ANNA:** A play, a song, an exhibition can open a window into another life, or our own.
**TOM:** And third, because it's simply one of the joys of life. We all need beauty and wonder.
**ANNA:** And much of it is affordable, or even free, if you know where to look.
**TOM:** Which is the whole point of the portal... making it easy to find and to join in.
**ANNA:** Let's make it practical. How can someone use the culture portal?
**TOM:** First, before a weekend, check the agenda for events near you, and try something new.
**ANNA:** Second, look up the museums and venues in your region, and plan a visit.
**TOM:** Third, watch for the big shared moments, like museum nights and heritage days.
**ANNA:** And fourth, if you're an artist or organiser, explore how the sector is supported.
**TOM:** Let's bring it together. What should people remember about the culture portal?
**ANNA:** First... culture dot lu gathers Luxembourg's cultural life in one place, supported by the Ministry of Culture.
**TOM:** Second... its agenda lists events across the whole country, for every taste.
**ANNA:** Third... it points you to museums, venues and heritage, and to special open days.
**TOM:** And fourth... it supports the cultural sector and aims to make culture accessible to all.
**ANNA:** Culture is the soul of a country... the songs it sings, the stories it tells, the beauty it shares.
**TOM:** And it belongs to everyone. So go and enjoy your share of it.
**ANNA:** Thank you so much for listening today.
**TOM:** Take care, go and discover something, and see you in the next episode. Äddi!
**ANNA:** Äddi!

*Sources: culture.lu — the cultural portal supported by the Ministry of Culture, grouping together essential information on cultural life in Luxembourg: an agenda of cultural events across the country, listings of cultural venues and institutions (museums, theatres, cultural centres), heritage (including events such as museum nights and European heritage days), and support for the cultural sector within Luxembourg's cultural development plan and cooperation with the communes. General information only. Accurate as of June 2026.*
