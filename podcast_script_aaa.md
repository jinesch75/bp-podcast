# Podcast Script — "The Accident Insurance (AAA) – Cover If You Are Hurt at Work or School"

**Part of the Biergerpakt programme**
**Hosts:** Anna (woman) and Tom (man)
**Length:** about 13 minutes — spoken slowly, in simple English

---

**ANNA:** Hello, and welcome back! It's good to have you with us today.
**TOM:** Hello everyone!
**ANNA:** This episode is part of the Biergerpakt. That is a programme about living together in Luxembourg. The idea is simple... we want to help residents, and cross-border workers too, to know their rights and protections.
**TOM:** And today's topic is reassuring to understand before you ever need it... what happens if you get hurt at work, or on the way there.
**ANNA:** Or even if your child has an accident at school.
**TOM:** For all of that, Luxembourg has a special part of its social security... the Accident Insurance Association.
**ANNA:** People know it by its initials in French... AAA, the Association d'assurance accident.
**TOM:** So Anna, what exactly is the AAA?
**ANNA:** It is the body that runs Luxembourg's insurance against accidents at work, and occupational diseases.
**TOM:** It's part of the social security system. So it's public, and it's there for you automatically.
**ANNA:** Let's be clear about what it covers, because it's broader than people think.
**TOM:** The first is the accident at work itself... something that happens to you while doing your job.
**ANNA:** The second is the commuting accident... an accident on your normal way to or from work.
**TOM:** So even before you arrive at the office, you're protected on that journey.
**ANNA:** The third is accidents at school, and around school activities, for pupils and students.
**TOM:** So a child who is injured during school is covered too. That's a real comfort for parents.
**ANNA:** And the fourth is occupational diseases... illnesses caused by your work over time.
**TOM:** For example, certain conditions linked to particular jobs or exposures.
**ANNA:** Now, the big question. If something happens, what does the AAA actually do for you?
**TOM:** It takes care of the costs and the consequences of the accident.
**ANNA:** That can include your medical care, and the costs of getting better.
**TOM:** If you can't work for a while, or if there are lasting effects, there can be financial compensation.
**ANNA:** And in the most serious cases, including the loss of a life, there is support for the family.
**TOM:** So the AAA cushions the blow, so that an accident doesn't also become a financial disaster.
**ANNA:** Let's talk about how it's paid for, because that's interesting.
**TOM:** The insurance is funded mainly by employers, through contributions.
**ANNA:** And there is a bonus-malus system... companies with fewer accidents can pay less.
**TOM:** That's clever. It rewards workplaces that take safety seriously.
**ANNA:** Which brings us to something just as important as paying out... preventing accidents in the first place.
**TOM:** The AAA does a lot on workplace safety and health.
**ANNA:** It gives recommendations, training, and advice to help workplaces be safer.
**TOM:** There's even a vision behind it, sometimes called "Vision Zero"... aiming for zero serious and fatal accidents.
**ANNA:** Because the best accident is the one that never happens.
**TOM:** Now let's make this practical. What should you actually do if an accident happens?
**ANNA:** The key thing is that the accident must be declared to the AAA.
**TOM:** For accidents at work, your employer normally makes that declaration.
**ANNA:** And much of this can now be done electronically, through MyGuichet, which we covered in an earlier episode.
**TOM:** So the system is becoming simpler and faster.
**ANNA:** It's also worth knowing the AAA is there to answer questions, with a contact service and a counter.
**TOM:** So you're not alone in figuring out a stressful situation.
**ANNA:** Let's step back. Why does this matter, even when life is going well?
**TOM:** Because accidents are, by definition, things we don't plan for. And knowing you're covered brings peace of mind.
**ANNA:** It means a worker, a commuter, or a child at school is protected, automatically, by society.
**TOM:** It's solidarity in action... we all contribute, so that anyone who is hurt is supported.
**ANNA:** Let's make it practical. What should people take from this?
**TOM:** First, know that accidents at work, on the way to work, and at school are covered by the AAA.
**ANNA:** Second, if an accident happens, make sure it's declared... usually your employer or the school helps with that.
**TOM:** Third, take prevention seriously. The AAA's advice can help keep your workplace safe.
**ANNA:** Let's bring it together. What should people remember about the AAA?
**TOM:** First... it's Luxembourg's public accident insurance, part of social security.
**ANNA:** Second... it covers accidents at work, commuting accidents, school accidents, and occupational diseases.
**TOM:** Third... it pays for care and compensation, and it works hard on prevention and workplace safety.
**ANNA:** And fourth... accidents must be declared, increasingly through MyGuichet.
**TOM:** We hope you never need it. But it's good to know it's there, quietly protecting you.
**ANNA:** That's what social security is... a safety net we weave together, for one another.
**TOM:** Thank you so much for listening today.
**ANNA:** Take care, stay safe, and see you in the next episode. Äddi!
**TOM:** Äddi!

*Sources: Association d'assurance accident (AAA), aaa.public.lu — part of Luxembourg's social security ("Cité de la sécurité sociale"); cover for accidents at work, commuting (trajet) accidents, school and extracurricular accidents, and occupational diseases; benefits (medical care, compensation/rentes) funded by employer contributions with a bonus-malus system; occupational safety and health prevention (recommendations, training, the "Vision Zero" approach); declaration of accidents, increasingly via MyGuichet.lu. General information only — for your situation, contact the AAA. Accurate as of June 2026.*
