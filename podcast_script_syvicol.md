# Podcast Script — "SYVICOL – The Voice of Luxembourg's Towns and Communes"

**Part of the Biergerpakt programme**
**Hosts:** Anna (woman) and Tom (man)
**Length:** about 13 minutes — spoken slowly, in simple English

---

**ANNA:** Hello, and welcome back! It's a pleasure to have you with us today.
**TOM:** Hello everyone!
**ANNA:** This episode is part of the Biergerpakt. That is a programme about living together in Luxembourg. The idea is simple... we want to help residents, and cross-border workers too, to understand how the country is run.
**TOM:** And today we are looking at a level of government that is closest to your daily life... your commune.
**ANNA:** Your commune is your town or village administration. It looks after so many everyday things.
**TOM:** Schools, local roads, water, waste collection, parks, sports halls, civil records... the list is long.
**ANNA:** Luxembourg has one hundred communes, from the city of Luxembourg to small rural villages.
**TOM:** And today's organisation speaks for all of them, together. It is called SYVICOL.
**ANNA:** SYVICOL stands for the Syndicat des Villes et Communes Luxembourgeoises.
**TOM:** In English, that's roughly the Association of Luxembourg Towns and Communes.
**ANNA:** So Tom, what exactly is SYVICOL, and why does it exist?
**TOM:** It is the organisation that brings together all of Luxembourg's communes into one common voice.
**ANNA:** Its job is to promote, protect and defend the shared interests of the communes.
**TOM:** Think about it. Each commune is fairly small. On its own, its voice might be quiet.
**ANNA:** But together, through SYVICOL, the hundred communes can speak clearly and with real weight.
**TOM:** And who do they speak to, mainly?
**ANNA:** Above all, to the national government. SYVICOL is the government's main partner for anything that affects the communes.
**TOM:** So when the State plans a new law that touches local life, SYVICOL is there to represent the towns and villages.
**ANNA:** Let's talk about one of its most important roles... giving its opinion on new laws.
**TOM:** When a draft law concerns the communes, SYVICOL studies it and gives a formal opinion.
**ANNA:** For example, on rules about water, about schools, about local finances, or about how communes are organised.
**TOM:** That opinion helps lawmakers understand the real, practical effects on the ground.
**ANNA:** Because the people running a commune know what will actually work in a real town or village.
**TOM:** So SYVICOL is a bridge... carrying local experience up to national decisions.
**ANNA:** It also works the other way. It helps communes share knowledge and good practice with each other.
**TOM:** A small commune facing a new challenge doesn't have to invent everything alone.
**ANNA:** Through SYVICOL, communes can consult each other, and learn from what others have tried.
**TOM:** There's also support for the people who actually run the communes... the local councillors.
**ANNA:** Yes. Being an elected local representative is a big responsibility, and SYVICOL helps with information and training.
**TOM:** And SYVICOL represents Luxembourg's communes beyond the borders too.
**ANNA:** At the European level, towns and regions from across Europe work together, and Luxembourg's communes have a voice there as well.
**TOM:** So local concerns can be heard not just nationally, but internationally.
**ANNA:** Let's step back and ask... why does this matter for an ordinary resident?
**TOM:** Because the commune is where democracy is closest to you. It shapes your street, your school, your daily surroundings.
**ANNA:** And SYVICOL helps make sure that this local level is respected and well represented when big decisions are made.
**TOM:** It's a quiet but important part of how the country stays balanced... between the national and the local.
**ANNA:** It also connects to something we care about in the Biergerpakt... taking part in local life.
**TOM:** Yes. Your commune is one of the easiest places to get involved, to be heard, and to make a difference.
**ANNA:** Voting in local elections, joining a commission, going to a public meeting... it all shapes your community.
**TOM:** And behind the scenes, SYVICOL is working so that all those communes have the tools and the voice they need.
**ANNA:** Let's make it practical. What can someone take from this?
**TOM:** First, remember that your commune handles a huge part of your daily life... and you can engage with it directly.
**ANNA:** Second, know that all the communes act together, through SYVICOL, when national decisions affect local life.
**TOM:** Third, if you're curious about how towns and villages are governed, SYVICOL is the place that represents them all.
**ANNA:** Let's bring it together. What should people remember about SYVICOL?
**TOM:** First... it is the association that unites all one hundred of Luxembourg's communes into one common voice.
**ANNA:** Second... it promotes and defends their shared interests, and is the government's main partner on communal matters.
**TOM:** Third... it gives formal opinions on laws that affect the communes, carrying local experience into national decisions.
**ANNA:** And fourth... it helps communes share knowledge, supports local councillors, and represents them in Europe.
**TOM:** The commune is where the country meets your front door. And SYVICOL helps keep that level strong.
**ANNA:** Strong local communities are the building blocks of a country where people truly live together.
**TOM:** Thank you so much for listening today.
**ANNA:** Take care, and see you in the next episode. Äddi!
**TOM:** Äddi!

*Sources: SYVICOL (Syndicat des Villes et Communes Luxembourgeoises), syvicol.lu — "Who are we", the statutes, and its role and opinions. SYVICOL brings together all 100 communes of Luxembourg to promote, safeguard and defend their general and collective interests; it is the government's privileged interlocutor for the communal sector, gives formal opinions on draft legislation affecting the communes, fosters consultation between members, supports local elected representatives, and represents the communes nationally and internationally. General information only. Accurate as of June 2026.*
