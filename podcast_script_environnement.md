# Podcast Script — "The Environment Portal – emwelt.lu and Caring for Nature in Luxembourg"

**Part of the Biergerpakt programme**
**Hosts:** Anna (woman) and Tom (man)
**Length:** about 14 minutes — spoken slowly, in simple English

---

**ANNA:** Hello, and welcome back! It's good to have you with us today.
**TOM:** Hello everyone!
**ANNA:** This episode is part of the Biergerpakt. That is a programme about living together in Luxembourg. The idea is simple... we want to help residents, and cross-border workers too, to feel connected to the country and to its nature.
**TOM:** And today's topic is one we all share, wherever we come from... the environment.
**ANNA:** The air we breathe, the water we drink, the forests we walk in, the waste we produce.
**TOM:** These can feel like big, distant subjects. But there is a single place that brings them all together for citizens.
**ANNA:** It is the environment portal of Luxembourg. Its friendly name is emwelt dot lu.
**TOM:** "Ëmwelt" is the Luxembourgish word for environment. So this is, simply, the environment portal.
**ANNA:** So Tom, who is behind it, and what is it for?
**TOM:** It is run by the Ministry of the Environment, Climate and Biodiversity.
**ANNA:** And its purpose is to inform the public about the environment and sustainable development, and to give access to environmental services.
**TOM:** Think of it as a single doorway into everything the State does to protect nature, and how you can take part.
**ANNA:** Let's walk through the main areas it covers, because it is wonderfully broad.
**TOM:** The first is nature... biodiversity, our forests, the soil, and the species that live here.
**ANNA:** The portal even has a whole section on the return of the wolf to Luxembourg, which is fascinating.
**TOM:** The second area is water... drinking water, rivers, groundwater, and wastewater.
**ANNA:** You can even check the quality of bathing water before you go for a swim in summer.
**TOM:** The third area is air and noise... the quality of the air we breathe, and noise pollution.
**ANNA:** And there is a real-time air-quality service, so you can see live measurements.
**TOM:** The fourth area is climate and energy... protecting the climate and adapting to its changes.
**ANNA:** Luxembourg has a national energy and climate plan, and a strategy to adapt to a warming world.
**TOM:** The fifth area is waste and resources... and this is where each of us can really make a difference.
**ANNA:** Yes! The portal explains how to sort your waste, and the country's zero-waste strategy, "Null Offall Lëtzebuerg".
**TOM:** There's even an app to help you know what goes in which bin.
**ANNA:** There are more areas too... chemicals and safe products, fishing and hunting, and protecting the soil.
**TOM:** So it really is a one-stop portal for the whole environment.
**ANNA:** Now, let's talk about the part I find most inviting... experiencing nature.
**TOM:** The portal points you to nature and forest centres, and to nature trails across the country.
**ANNA:** So it's not only about rules and protection. It's also an invitation to go outside and enjoy the natural world.
**TOM:** And enjoying nature is often the first step to wanting to protect it.
**ANNA:** The portal is also practical, with real services for citizens and professionals.
**TOM:** There is a section on environmental procedures... permits, financial aids, and official forms.
**ANNA:** So if you need an environmental permit, or a grant for a green project, you can find the way in here.
**TOM:** And there are useful alerts too... like reporting a pollution, or checking flood information.
**ANNA:** Let's connect this to two earlier episodes, because the picture fits together.
**TOM:** Yes. For climate and energy advice at home, there's Klima-Agence, which we covered.
**ANNA:** And for housing and land, there's the Observatoire de l'Habitat. The environment portal sits alongside them.
**TOM:** Together, they show a country thinking carefully about its future.
**ANNA:** Let's talk about why this matters for each of us, in daily life.
**TOM:** First, because the environment is not somewhere else. It is the air on your street and the water in your tap.
**ANNA:** Second, because small actions add up. Sorting waste, saving water, protecting a green space... it all counts.
**TOM:** And third, because being informed lets you take part... in consultations, in local projects, in everyday choices.
**ANNA:** The portal even highlights calls for projects to bring more nature into our towns and villages.
**TOM:** So this is not a passive website. It's an invitation to act, together.
**ANNA:** Let's make it practical. How can someone use emwelt dot lu?
**TOM:** First, before a summer swim, check the bathing-water quality. Before a high-pollution day, check the air.
**ANNA:** Second, when in doubt about waste, use the sorting guide or the app to recycle correctly.
**TOM:** Third, if you have a green project, or need an environmental permit, look up the procedures and grants.
**ANNA:** And fourth, to reconnect with nature, find a nearby nature trail or forest centre and simply go.
**TOM:** Let's bring it all together. What should people remember about the environment portal?
**ANNA:** First... emwelt dot lu is Luxembourg's one-stop environment portal, from the Ministry of the Environment, Climate and Biodiversity.
**TOM:** Second... it covers nature, water, air, climate, waste, and more, with real-time information and services.
**ANNA:** Third... it's both protective and inviting... rules and permits, but also nature trails and discovery.
**TOM:** And fourth... it shows how each of us can take part, with simple daily actions and local projects.
**ANNA:** Nature gives us so much, quietly, every day. Caring for it is a way of caring for each other.
**TOM:** And it starts with knowing, and then with small, steady steps.
**ANNA:** Thank you so much for listening today.
**TOM:** Take care of yourselves and of our shared home. See you in the next episode. Äddi!
**ANNA:** Äddi!
