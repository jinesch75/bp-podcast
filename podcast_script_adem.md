# Podcast Script — "ADEM – Luxembourg's Employment Agency, and How It Can Help You"

**Part of the Biergerpakt programme**
**Hosts:** Anna (woman) and Tom (man)
**Length:** about 15 minutes — spoken slowly, in simple English

---

**ANNA:** Hello, and welcome! It's lovely to have you with us again today.
**TOM:** Hello everyone!
**ANNA:** This episode is part of the Biergerpakt. That is a programme about living together in Luxembourg. The idea is simple... we want to help residents, and cross-border workers too, to discover the country, and to feel more at home here.
**TOM:** And today we are talking about something that touches almost everyone at some point in their life... work.
**ANNA:** More precisely, we are talking about ADEM.
**TOM:** ADEM. You may have seen the name, maybe on a letter, or near one of their offices. But what exactly is it?
**ANNA:** ADEM stands for the "Agence pour le développement de l'emploi". In English, that means the Employment Development Agency.
**TOM:** It is Luxembourg's public employment service. Think of it as the official agency of the State that helps people find work, and helps employers find staff.
**ANNA:** Its motto is nice and clear... "Facilitons l'emploi". Let's make employment easier.
**TOM:** So ADEM sits in the middle, between people who are looking for a job, and companies that are looking for people.
**ANNA:** Exactly. And it is a public service, which means it is free to use.
**TOM:** Good to know. So Anna, who is ADEM actually for?
**ANNA:** Mainly three groups. First, jobseekers... people who are looking for work, or who want to change jobs.
**TOM:** Second, employees... people who already have a job but want advice or training.
**ANNA:** And third, employers... companies and organisations that want to hire.
**TOM:** So it really is for a lot of people. Even if you have a job right now, ADEM might still be relevant one day.
**ANNA:** That's the key message. You don't have to wait until you are in difficulty. ADEM is there before, during, and after a job search.
**TOM:** Let's talk about the heart of it... registering as a jobseeker. Why would someone do that?
**ANNA:** When you register, you officially say... I am looking for work, and I am available for the job market.
**TOM:** And being "available for the job market" has a real meaning, doesn't it?
**ANNA:** It does. It means you can be reached easily by ADEM and by employers, and that you are ready to work for the hours you have indicated.
**TOM:** So you register, and then a door opens to all of ADEM's help.
**ANNA:** Yes. Once you are registered, you get a personal advisor, access to job offers, training, and support with your search.
**TOM:** Let's slow down on the personal advisor, because I think that is really important.
**ANNA:** It is. After your registration is approved, you are invited to a first appointment with an ADEM counsellor.
**TOM:** This counsellor becomes your main point of contact during your whole job search.
**ANNA:** They follow your situation personally. They help you plan your steps, and they stay with you along the way.
**TOM:** So you are not alone in front of a website. There is a real person who knows your file.
**ANNA:** Exactly. Now, how do you actually register? There are two ways.
**TOM:** The first way is online, and this is the modern, easy route.
**ANNA:** You register on MyGuichet dot lu. That is the State's secure online platform that we talked about in an earlier episode.
**TOM:** The platform guides you step by step. You give information about your profile, and the kind of job you are looking for.
**ANNA:** You can also upload your documents directly, so everything is in one place.
**TOM:** And to log in, you need a secure authentication device, like a LuxTrust product.
**ANNA:** That is the same secure login you use for many official services in Luxembourg.
**TOM:** What if someone prefers to meet a person, face to face?
**ANNA:** Then there is the second way... registering in person at an ADEM agency.
**TOM:** For that, you first call to make an appointment. The number is the ADEM Contact Centre, on 2-4-7, 8-8-8-8-8.
**ANNA:** And ADEM has three locations... in Luxembourg City, in Esch-Belval, and in Diekirch. You'll be offered the office closest to your home.
**TOM:** Good. So whether you are more comfortable online or in person, both doors are open.
**ANNA:** Now, Tom, what should someone bring or prepare before registering?
**TOM:** A few simple things. First, your social security number, which is on your social security card.
**ANNA:** Make sure your identity documents are valid... your ID card, passport, or residence permit.
**TOM:** And bring your CV, up to date, with your experience, your skills, your training, and the languages you speak.
**ANNA:** If you have certificates from vocational training, those are a real plus too.
**TOM:** And here is a detail many people don't know. You can register with ADEM even if you still have a job.
**ANNA:** Yes! As long as you meet ADEM's requirements and you can attend the appointments, you don't have to wait until you are unemployed.
**TOM:** Let's talk about what you actually get once you are registered. You mentioned the JobBoard.
**ANNA:** The JobBoard is ADEM's platform of job offers. As a registered jobseeker, you get access to browse vacancies and send applications.
**TOM:** You can also upload your CV there, so employers can find you.
**ANNA:** Then there are training courses and employment measures, chosen according to your profile and your needs.
**TOM:** And there is something called the Employment Club, right?
**ANNA:** Yes. There you can use computers and get help preparing your CV. Practical, hands-on support.
**TOM:** So registration is not just a formality. It unlocks real, concrete help.
**ANNA:** Exactly. But of course, with help come some responsibilities too.
**TOM:** That's fair. When you register, you are saying you will actively look for work.
**ANNA:** So you are expected to take steps on your own... reply to job adverts, send applications, search the JobBoard, keep your CV updated.
**TOM:** You also follow the plan you build together with your advisor, and you attend your appointments.
**ANNA:** And you tell ADEM about changes that affect your availability... a move, a new phone number, the start of a job, an illness, and so on.
**TOM:** It's a two-way relationship. ADEM supports you, and you stay active and in contact.
**ANNA:** And there is a nice detail... as a jobseeker you are entitled to a number of leave days each year, up to twenty-five working days.
**TOM:** During that approved leave you are exempt from your obligations to ADEM, a bit like holidays. You just have to request it in advance.
**ANNA:** Now let's talk about money, because many people come to ADEM for this... unemployment benefit.
**TOM:** In French, the "indemnité de chômage". This is financial support if you have lost your job.
**ANNA:** And here is the important rule. To apply for unemployment benefit, you must first be registered as a jobseeker with ADEM.
**TOM:** Once your jobseeker file is open, ADEM checks automatically whether you may be entitled to benefits, based on your situation.
**ANNA:** So registration comes first... then the benefit question follows.
**TOM:** And there is good news on how you apply. Recently, applying for unemployment benefit became fully online.
**ANNA:** That means less paperwork, and time saved. You can do more of it from home.
**TOM:** Let's give some attention to younger listeners now, because Luxembourg has a special promise for them.
**ANNA:** It's called the Youth Guarantee. The goal is that every young person, aged fifteen to thirty, is offered a concrete solution to enter working life.
**TOM:** And there's a clear timeframe... within four months of registering with ADEM.
**ANNA:** That solution might be a job, but it could also be training, or another step that moves you forward.
**TOM:** So if you are young and unsure what to do next, ADEM is a very good place to start.
**ANNA:** ADEM also has dedicated support for people with a disability, or with reduced work capacity.
**TOM:** There are special measures, internships, financial benefits, and personal guidance, to help people find or return to work.
**ANNA:** The goal is inclusion... making sure that everyone has a fair chance in the job market.
**TOM:** Let's not forget the other side of ADEM... the employers.
**ANNA:** Right. In Luxembourg, employers are actually required to declare their vacant positions to ADEM.
**TOM:** And in return, they get help. ADEM connects them with candidates from its pool of jobseekers.
**ANNA:** Employers can also receive financial aid when they hire certain people... for example a young jobseeker, someone who has been unemployed for a long time, or a person with a disability.
**TOM:** So ADEM doesn't just help individuals. It helps the whole job market work better.
**ANNA:** There is also support for international recruitment, through a service called Work in Luxembourg.
**TOM:** That helps companies here find talent, and helps people abroad discover opportunities in the country.
**ANNA:** Tom, before we finish, there is one safety point we really should mention.
**TOM:** Yes, an important one. Sometimes scammers send fake emails pretending to be ADEM, and they ask for money for a job or a visa.
**ANNA:** Please remember this clearly... ADEM never asks you to pay for a job or a visa. The service is free.
**TOM:** If anyone asks you for payment in ADEM's name, it is a scam. Don't pay, and don't share your details.
**ANNA:** One more practical thing... languages. To deal with ADEM, you should speak at least one of Luxembourg's administrative languages... Luxembourgish, French, or German, or else English.
**TOM:** And if you don't, you can bring someone you trust who does, to help you during your appointments.
**ANNA:** So let's bring it all together. What should people remember about ADEM?
**TOM:** First... ADEM is Luxembourg's free, public employment service, for jobseekers, employees, and employers.
**ANNA:** Second... registering as a jobseeker opens the door to a personal advisor, the JobBoard, training, and support.
**TOM:** Third... you register online on MyGuichet dot lu with a secure login, or in person by appointment in Luxembourg, Esch-Belval, or Diekirch.
**ANNA:** Fourth... to claim unemployment benefit, you must register first, and applying is now fully online.
**TOM:** And fifth... there is special help for young people, through the Youth Guarantee, and for people with disabilities.
**ANNA:** And remember the safety rule... ADEM never asks for payment.
**TOM:** A job search can feel stressful. But the message of this episode is that you don't have to do it alone.
**ANNA:** ADEM is there to walk beside you. One step at a time.
**TOM:** Thank you for listening, and take care.
**ANNA:** See you in the next episode. Äddi!
