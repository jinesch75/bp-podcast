# Podcast Script — "Work in Luxembourg: One Front Door to a New Life"

**Part of the Biergerpakt programme**
**Hosts:** Anna (woman) and Tom (man)
**Length:** about 14 minutes — spoken slowly, in simple English

---

**ANNA:** Hello, and welcome! It's really nice to have you with us today.

**TOM:** Hello everyone!

**ANNA:** This episode is part of the Biergerpakt. That's a programme of living together in Luxembourg. The idea is simple... we want to help residents, and also cross-border workers, to discover Luxembourg — or re-discover it — and to meet new people.

**TOM:** Exactly. And today's topic is about that very journey — how people come to work and live in Luxembourg in the first place. It's about an official portal called Work in Luxembourg.

**ANNA:** Work in Luxembourg. Tom, let's start at the beginning. What is it?

**TOM:** Work in Luxembourg is an official, government-led website. Its job is to be a single front door — one place that brings together everything you need to know about working and living in Luxembourg.

**ANNA:** A single front door. Because normally, that information is scattered everywhere.

**TOM:** Exactly. Think about someone abroad who's curious about moving here. Where do they even start? Jobs are on one site, visas on another, housing somewhere else, schools somewhere else again. It's overwhelming.

**ANNA:** So Work in Luxembourg gathers it all in one place.

**TOM:** One place, in clear, consistent information. The address is simple — workinluxembourg dot com.

**ANNA:** And who is it really for? Only people living abroad?

**TOM:** Mainly, yes — it's aimed at international talent. People around the world with skills, who might want to build a career and a life here. But it's also genuinely useful for anyone curious about the country — and for employers who want to hire from abroad.

**ANNA:** Now you said "government-led". Who exactly is behind it?

**TOM:** It's a real team effort by the country. It was built together by the Ministry of the Economy, the Ministry of Labour, ADEM — which is the national employment agency — the Chamber of Commerce, the Chamber of Skilled Trades, and an agency called Luxinnovation.

**ANNA:** So that's a lot of official partners pulling together.

**TOM:** All rowing in the same direction. And there's a clear reason behind it. Luxembourg, like many places, has a shortage of qualified people in certain jobs. So the country wants to attract talent — and to welcome them well.

**ANNA:** Let's get practical. If I open the website, what do I actually find? How is it organised?

**TOM:** Beautifully simply. There are three main sections. They're called "Work here", "Live here", and "Get started".

**ANNA:** Work here, Live here, Get started. Let's take them one by one. Start with "Work here".

**TOM:** "Work here" is about the professional side. It explains the Luxembourg job market, the key economic sectors, the kinds of careers available, and the working conditions. So you can understand what your professional life might actually look like.

**ANNA:** So before you even pack a bag, you understand the world of work you'd be entering.

**TOM:** Exactly. What industries are strong, what your rights as a worker are, what a career path could be.

**ANNA:** Okay — "Live here". I'm guessing that's about everyday life?

**TOM:** Precisely. "Live here" is about quality of life. Housing, family, education, healthcare, culture, leisure — and the multicultural, multilingual environment. Because a job is only half of a life. The other half is — will my family be happy here?

**ANNA:** That's so important. People don't just move for a job. They move for a life.

**TOM:** They move for a life. And this section helps you picture it — schools for the children, the health system, things to do at the weekend.

**ANNA:** And the third one — "Get started".

**TOM:** "Get started" is the practical, roll-up-your-sleeves section. It covers immigration — residence and work permits — plus job hunting, housing, and a full relocation guide. It's the "how do I actually do this" part.

**ANNA:** So the first two sections inspire you, and the third one helps you take real steps.

**TOM:** That's the perfect way to say it. Dream it in "Work here" and "Live here", then do it in "Get started". And for companies, there's even an Employer Toolkit, to help them recruit from abroad.

**ANNA:** Now, Tom — the big one for most people. Jobs. Can you actually find a job through this portal?

**TOM:** You can. There's a Job Board — a place to search for open positions. And here's the clever part — it's connected directly to ADEM's international recruitment platform.

**ANNA:** ADEM — that's the national employment agency you mentioned.

**TOM:** Right. ADEM is Luxembourg's public employment service. So the jobs aren't random — they come through the official agency. The listings are updated daily, and you can create a profile and apply directly to vacancies.

**ANNA:** And does anyone actually look at those profiles? Or do they just disappear into a database?

**TOM:** They're real. In fact, the platform already has around eleven thousand candidates from all over the world, pre-selected by ADEM. So it's an active pool that employers really use to find people.

**ANNA:** Eleven thousand. So it's a living marketplace, not an empty noticeboard.

**TOM:** A living marketplace. Employers looking for skills, and skilled people looking for a future — meeting in one place.

**ANNA:** You mentioned that Luxembourg has shortages in certain jobs. Does the portal help with that specifically?

**TOM:** Yes — and this is really useful to know. Luxembourg publishes a list of "shortage occupations" — the jobs where the country most needs people. And for professionals from outside the European Union, working in one of those shortage jobs can mean faster immigration procedures.

**ANNA:** So if your skill is one the country urgently needs, the door opens more quickly.

**TOM:** More quickly and more smoothly. It's a way of saying — we really need you, so let's make it easier.

**ANNA:** Now, all of this online information is wonderful. But moving country is stressful. Is there a human you can actually talk to?

**TOM:** There is — and I love this part. Alongside the website, there's something called the Talent Desk.

**ANNA:** The Talent Desk. What is it?

**TOM:** It's a service that gives personalised, human support. It informs you, advises you, and guides you through the administrative steps and the integration process. And if you need a specific institution, it puts you in touch with the right one.

**ANNA:** So a real person, helping you find your way through the paperwork and the settling-in.

**TOM:** A real person. The website gives you the information; the Talent Desk gives you the guidance. And it doesn't only help individuals — it also supports companies with their international recruitment.

**ANNA:** So both sides — the talent and the employer — get a helping hand.

**TOM:** Both sides. It makes the whole thing smoother, and warmer.

**ANNA:** Tom, let's talk about the picture the portal paints of Luxembourg. What does it say makes the country attractive? And let's be honest that this is a promotional site.

**TOM:** Fair — it is a showcase, so let's take it as that, with the facts attributed. It highlights things like safety and stability, a diverse, multicultural population, a dynamic economy, and a family-friendly environment with strong schools and free public transport.

**ANNA:** And it backs these up with rankings, doesn't it?

**TOM:** It does, and it names the sources. For example — according to the Legatum Prosperity Index, Luxembourg is ranked the number one safest country for expats. According to the IMD World Talent Ranking, it's second in the world for attracting talent. And it points out a striking fact about the workforce.

**ANNA:** Which is?

**TOM:** That around seventy-four percent of Luxembourg's workforce are either foreign nationals or cross-border commuters. And more than two hundred and twenty-eight thousand people cross a border every day to work here.

**ANNA:** Seventy-four percent. So working in an international team isn't the exception here — it's completely normal.

**TOM:** It's the everyday reality. People in Luxembourg speak, on average, several languages — Luxembourgish, French, German, and a lot of English at work. For an international newcomer, that's reassuring. You're not the odd one out. Everyone came from somewhere.

**ANNA:** That's a lovely thought. Let's make it concrete. Can we follow one person?

**TOM:** Let's. Imagine a software engineer — let's call her Priya. She lives far away, and she's heard Luxembourg might be a good place for her skills. But she has no idea where to begin.

**ANNA:** The classic situation. So many questions, and no map.

**TOM:** So she finds the portal — workinluxembourg.com. First, she reads "Work here", and sees that technology is a strong, growing sector. Then she opens "Live here", and looks at schools and housing for her family.

**ANNA:** So far she's just exploring, getting a feel for it.

**TOM:** Exactly. Then she goes to the Job Board, creates a profile, and applies to a couple of openings — real jobs, through ADEM. Maybe her field is even on the shortage list, which means a smoother immigration path.

**ANNA:** And when the paperwork feels overwhelming?

**TOM:** She turns to the Talent Desk, and a real person helps her understand the permits and the steps. And in "Get started", she follows the relocation guide — housing, registration, all of it.

**ANNA:** So from one website, she goes from "I have no idea" to "I have a job offer and a plan".

**TOM:** From a vague dream to a concrete move. That's the entire purpose of the portal — to turn confusion into a clear path.

**ANNA:** And honestly, this is useful even for people already here, isn't it?

**TOM:** Very much. If you have a friend or family member abroad who's thinking of joining you in Luxembourg, this is exactly where you send them. And if you're an employer struggling to find skills, this is where you look, and who you ask for help.

**ANNA:** Okay Tom, let's bring it home. If I'm listening, what should I take away?

**TOM:** A few simple things. First — if you, or someone you know, is thinking about working in Luxembourg, there's now one official front door. workinluxembourg.com. Start there, instead of getting lost.

**ANNA:** One front door.

**TOM:** Second — it's built around three clear sections. "Work here" for the career, "Live here" for the life, and "Get started" for the practical steps and permits.

**ANNA:** And third?

**TOM:** Third — you don't have to do it alone. The Job Board connects you to real jobs through ADEM, and the Talent Desk gives you a real human to guide you. Information, plus a helping hand.

**ANNA:** A short summary to finish?

**TOM:** Sure. Work in Luxembourg, at workinluxembourg.com, is an official, government-led portal — a single gateway for international talent who want to work and live in Luxembourg. It's organised in three sections — Work here, Live here, and Get started — and it's backed by the country's main economic and employment players, including ADEM. Its Job Board connects you to real vacancies through ADEM, and its Talent Desk offers personalised, human guidance. For shortage jobs, non-EU professionals can even get faster immigration.

**ANNA:** And the message for today?

**TOM:** That a country which depends on international talent has finally made one clear, welcoming door for it. If your future, or someone's you love, might be in Luxembourg — the path just got a lot simpler.

**ANNA:** So... whether it's for you, a friend abroad, or your own company — remember the address. Work in Luxembourg. workinluxembourg.com.

**TOM:** One front door, to a new life. That's a good thing to know about.

**ANNA:** That was our episode about the Work in Luxembourg portal, part of the Biergerpakt — living together in Luxembourg, discovering the country, and looking out for each other. Thank you so much for listening.

**TOM:** Thank you, everyone. Take care... and see you next time!

**ANNA:** Bye bye!

Three sections: "Work here", "Live here" and "Get started" (including a relocation guide and an Employer Toolkit), plus the Talent Desk, which provides personalised support to talent and companies.
