# Podcast Script — "The Maison de l'Orientation: One Place to Help You Choose Your Path"

**Part of the Biergerpakt programme**
**Hosts:** Anna (woman) and Tom (man)
**Length:** about 10 minutes — spoken slowly, in simple English

---

**ANNA:** Hello, and welcome! It's really nice to have you with us today.

**TOM:** Hello everyone!

**ANNA:** This episode is part of the Biergerpakt. That's a programme of living together in Luxembourg. The idea is simple... we want to help residents, and also cross-border workers, to discover Luxembourg — or re-discover it — and to meet new people.

**TOM:** Exactly. And today we talk about a place that helps you with one of life's big questions — what should I study, or what work should I do? It's called the Maison de l'Orientation.

**ANNA:** The Maison de l'Orientation. Let's translate that first, Tom. What does the name mean?

**TOM:** So, "Maison" means "house". And "Orientation" here means guidance — finding your direction, in school and in work. So you could call it "the House of Guidance". A house where you go to get help choosing your path.

**ANNA:** I like that. A house of guidance. So what exactly is it?

**TOM:** It's a State administration — an official public service. And its special idea is this: it brings together, in one single place, many public services that all work on school and career guidance.

**ANNA:** Ah, so instead of running around to ten different offices...

**TOM:** ...you go to one address, and you find them all under one roof. That's really the whole point. One place, many experts.

**ANNA:** And who is it for? Only students?

**TOM:** Not only. It's for any citizen who is looking for advice about their school path or their career. The goal is to help you identify your abilities, your skills and your interests — and then make good, informed decisions about your studies, your training, and your work plans.

**ANNA:** So it's about understanding yourself first, and then choosing.

**TOM:** Exactly. Know what you're good at, know what you like, and then choose with confidence.

**ANNA:** Okay. Let's get practical. If I just walk in, what do I find?

**TOM:** The heart of it is the "Espace Orientation" — the guidance space. It's a welcome area where you can come with your questions about school or vocational training.

**ANNA:** And is there an age for this part?

**TOM:** The guidance space especially welcomes people aged 14 to 22. So — teenagers and young adults. But, as we'll see, the house also has services for adults.

**ANNA:** Good. And do I need an appointment to use this guidance space?

**TOM:** No, you don't have to. And this is a lovely part — you can simply walk in, without an appointment, and get advice that is free, and anonymous.

**ANNA:** Free and anonymous. So nobody writes down my name, and it costs nothing.

**TOM:** Nothing at all. The only thing — if you walk in without an appointment, you might have to wait a little, because the people with appointments are seen first.

**ANNA:** That makes sense. So I can book, to skip the wait?

**TOM:** Yes. You can book an appointment, and it's done online — through MyGuichet.lu.

**ANNA:** MyGuichet again! We did a whole episode on that.

**TOM:** *(laughs)* Yes, it keeps coming back, because it's the door to so many services. On MyGuichet, you choose the format that suits you — an appointment online, by video, or an appointment in person, at the Maison de l'Orientation. And you can book with or without a login.

**ANNA:** Very flexible. And where is the house, exactly?

**TOM:** It's in Luxembourg City — 29, rue Aldringen. The guidance space is open Monday to Friday, from 8 in the morning to 5 in the afternoon. And there's a free phone line — 8002, 8181 — and an email, info at m-o dot l-u.

**ANNA:** Good. Now, you said they give advice. On what topics, concretely?

**TOM:** Two big families of topics. The first is your school path. So — guidance about secondary school, what the different lycées offer, the choice of sections, or how to get back into a lycée. The second is training and trades. So — information about jobs and professions, about first work experiences, about courses, and about the apprenticeship.

**ANNA:** So from "which school section should I choose" to "how do I start an apprenticeship".

**TOM:** Exactly. The whole journey from school into the world of work.

**ANNA:** Now you mentioned a special session — coaching?

**TOM:** Yes. They offer a guidance coaching session, together with an interests test. The test helps you discover what really motivates you, what kind of activities suit you. This one is for students, it's voluntary, and for this you do need an appointment.

**ANNA:** An interests test — that sounds genuinely useful when you have no idea what to do.

**TOM:** It really helps. Many young people don't know where to start, and a test like this gives a first direction to explore.

**ANNA:** Okay, Tom. You said many services are under one roof. Can you tell us who they are?

**TOM:** Yes, and this is the strength of the house. Several public services sit there, and you can make an appointment with them all year — even during the school holidays — Monday to Friday. Let me name the main ones.

**ANNA:** Please.

**TOM:** First, the CePAS — the psycho-social and school support centre. They help pupils with personal, social or school difficulties. Second, the SIA — the service for the integration and reception of pupils in school. This one is very important for newcomers: it helps foreign pupils find their place in the Luxembourg school system.

**ANNA:** That's directly useful for families arriving from abroad.

**TOM:** Very much. Then, third — the ADEM career guidance service, and apprenticeship placement. ADEM is the employment agency, and here they help with vocational orientation and finding an apprenticeship. Fourth — the ATVA, the agency for the transition to an autonomous life. They support young people with specific needs, to move towards independence.

**ANNA:** So there's something even for people who need extra support.

**TOM:** Yes. And then, fifth — Euroguidance. This is a European network, for those who dream of studying or working abroad. And finally — adult guidance. Because, as I promised, it's not only for the young.

**ANNA:** Let's stay on that — adults. Because many of our listeners are grown-ups, maybe thinking of changing job.

**TOM:** Right. So the Maison de l'Orientation also helps with changing your professional path. If you want to re-orient, or get back into work, they can guide you. If you want to go back to studying, or follow a training, they help. And if you arrived from another country, they help you with two key things — getting your diplomas recognised, and validating the experience you already have.

**ANNA:** That last point is so important for newcomers. You might have years of experience from your home country.

**TOM:** Exactly. And there's an official process to turn that experience into a recognised qualification here. The house can point you to it.

**ANNA:** Beautiful. So really, it covers the whole of life — a teenager choosing a school section, a young adult looking for an apprenticeship, and an adult changing career or arriving from abroad.

**TOM:** That's the idea. Guidance, for every stage.

**ANNA:** Now, Tom — there's also a website, isn't there? For people who want to look first, from home.

**TOM:** Yes, and it's a real resource on its own. The website is maison-orientation, all one word, dot public dot l-u. And it's organised around a few big themes. Let me walk through them.

**ANNA:** Please do.

**TOM:** The first theme is "My studies". There you find information about basic and secondary school, and a very practical thing — the calendar of open days of the lycées and the universities.

**ANNA:** Ah, the open days — when you can visit a school and see it for yourself.

**TOM:** Exactly. You go, you look around, you ask questions, you get a feeling for the place. And there's also a part called "What to do after the bac?" — the bac being the end-of-secondary diploma. So, what comes next — university, a higher technical diploma, and so on.

**ANNA:** Useful. What's the second theme?

**TOM:** The second is "The world of work". This is about discovering jobs and professions, and your first steps into working life. There's a special part for young people — about internships, holiday jobs, student jobs, even things like a gap year or "work and travel".

**ANNA:** Oh, so for a teenager who wants a summer job, or a first experience...

**TOM:** ...there's information made just for them. They even have a series of brochures, called BOOST, with exactly this kind of advice. And this theme also covers the apprenticeship, and the path "towards employment" — so, looking for a job.

**ANNA:** Good. And the third theme?

**TOM:** The third is "Changing your professional path". This is the one for adults we spoke about — help to re-orient or get back into work, going back to studies or following a training, and getting your diplomas recognised and your experience validated.

**ANNA:** And the fourth?

**TOM:** The fourth is "Help and support". This gathers the support for harder situations — what to do when you have difficulties, the financial and in-kind aid that exists, support for foreign pupils joining school, and support for people with specific needs.

**ANNA:** So the website almost mirrors the house — every kind of question has its place.

**TOM:** Exactly. And one more nice thing — the Maison de l'Orientation organises events and workshops all year. For example, workshops on how to write a CV — they even have one on making a video CV. Information evenings about studying and working abroad. And sessions for the parents of future apprentices.

**ANNA:** So there's an agenda of events too. Worth checking before you go.

**TOM:** Definitely. The agenda is on the website, with all the dates and places.

**ANNA:** Is there anything for people who are really stuck, or in difficulty?

**TOM:** Yes. The website has a whole section for support and help — including what to do when you have difficulties, the financial and in-kind aid that exists, support for foreign pupils, and support for people with specific needs. So you're never alone with the question.

**ANNA:** And all of this — still free?

**TOM:** The guidance and information service is free. That's the spirit of a public service like this.

**ANNA:** Okay, Tom. Let's make it really practical. If I'm listening, and I — or my child — have one of these questions, what do I do?

**TOM:** Very simple. If you want, book an appointment through MyGuichet.lu — choose online or in person. Or, if you prefer, just walk in to the Maison de l'Orientation, 29 rue Aldringen in Luxembourg City, Monday to Friday, between 8 and 5, for free and anonymous advice. And if you have a quick question, call the free line, 8002, 8181.

**ANNA:** Book on MyGuichet, or simply walk in. Easy. Short summary to finish?

**TOM:** Sure. The Maison de l'Orientation is a State house that brings many guidance services together in one place, in Luxembourg City. It helps you choose your school path or your career — by understanding your abilities and interests. The guidance space is free, and anonymous, mainly for people aged 14 to 22, but the house also helps adults and people changing career, and newcomers who need their diplomas or experience recognised.

**ANNA:** And how to start?

**TOM:** Book through MyGuichet.lu, or walk in, Monday to Friday. Free advice, and many experts under one roof.

**ANNA:** So... if you're not sure what to study, or what to do next — you don't have to figure it out alone. There's a whole house ready to help.

**TOM:** Really. Just go once, with your questions. You'll leave with a clearer path.

**ANNA:** That was our episode about the Maison de l'Orientation, part of the Biergerpakt — living together in Luxembourg, discovering the country, and finding your way. Thank you so much for listening.

**TOM:** Thank you, everyone. Take care... and see you next time!

**ANNA:** Bye bye!
