# Podcast Script — "Zesumme Vereinfachen – Helping to Simplify Public Services Together"

**Part of the Biergerpakt programme**
**Hosts:** Anna (woman) and Tom (man)
**Length:** about 14 minutes — spoken slowly, in simple English

---

**ANNA:** Hello, and welcome back! It's lovely to have you with us today.
**TOM:** Hello everyone!
**ANNA:** This episode is part of the Biergerpakt. That is a programme about living together in Luxembourg. The idea is simple... we want to help residents, and cross-border workers too, to take part in the life of the country.
**TOM:** And today we have a topic that, honestly, almost everyone has feelings about... paperwork.
**ANNA:** Yes! Forms, procedures, administrative steps. Sometimes they are simple. And sometimes... they are not.
**TOM:** We have all had that moment. A form that asks the same thing twice. A step that makes you think... why is this so complicated?
**ANNA:** Well, today's topic is a project that turns that frustration into something useful.
**TOM:** It is called Zesumme Vereinfachen. The name is in Luxembourgish.
**ANNA:** And it means "simplifying together". I love that. Together.
**TOM:** So Anna, what exactly is Zesumme Vereinfachen?
**ANNA:** It is an online platform where citizens and businesses can help make the State's procedures simpler.
**TOM:** So instead of just complaining about paperwork, you can actually do something about it.
**ANNA:** Exactly. Its slogan says it beautifully... public services that make your daily life easier.
**TOM:** And who is behind it?
**ANNA:** It is run by the Ministry for Digitalisation, through a body called the Simplification Council.
**TOM:** The platform was launched in 2022, and it is part of a bigger effort to simplify the administration.
**ANNA:** That bigger effort even has its own name... "Einfach Lëtzebuerg", which means "Simply Luxembourg".
**TOM:** Let's get to the heart of it. How does the platform actually work?
**ANNA:** The big idea is to put the real experience of citizens and businesses at the centre.
**TOM:** Because the people who use a procedure every day often understand its problems best.
**ANNA:** So citizens and businesses can take the initiative, and submit ideas to simplify the State's procedures.
**TOM:** You have noticed something clumsy, something that could be easier? You can suggest how to fix it.
**ANNA:** And then the work happens in a participatory, co-creative way... together with the people who proposed it.
**TOM:** I think it helps to picture it as a project that moves through phases.
**ANNA:** Yes. There is a phase for sharing ideas, and then phases for working on a solution and testing it.
**TOM:** And here is a detail I really appreciate. You don't have to commit to the whole journey.
**ANNA:** That's right. You can take part in just one phase, or several, as much as you want.
**TOM:** So you are free. You give what you can, when you can. No pressure.
**ANNA:** And the platform welcomes everyone, in all four of our languages... Luxembourgish, German, French, and English.
**TOM:** That really matters in a country as multilingual as Luxembourg. Everyone can join in their own language.
**ANNA:** Let's make this concrete. What kind of thing could be simplified?
**TOM:** Imagine a form that asks for information the State already has. Why fill it in again?
**ANNA:** Or a procedure that involves three different offices, when one would do.
**TOM:** Or instructions that are hard to understand, that could be written in plain language.
**ANNA:** A good example is digital services. The platform has been used to help improve the MyGuichet app.
**TOM:** And we have talked about MyGuichet in an earlier episode. It's the State's online counter.
**ANNA:** So real users gave feedback, and that feedback helped shape a tool that millions of procedures pass through.
**TOM:** That's the magic. One person's small suggestion can improve life for thousands.
**ANNA:** Let's talk about why this approach matters, beyond the paperwork itself.
**TOM:** First, it saves time. Simpler procedures mean less stress, for citizens and for staff.
**ANNA:** Second, it builds trust. When the State listens, people feel respected, and feel part of things.
**TOM:** And third, it is democratic, in a quiet, everyday way.
**ANNA:** Yes. Democracy is not only voting every few years. It is also helping to shape the services you use.
**TOM:** And that fits the spirit of the Biergerpakt perfectly... citizens and the State, working together.
**ANNA:** There is also something hopeful here. Frustration can be turned into improvement.
**TOM:** Instead of "this is annoying", it becomes "here is how we could make it better".
**ANNA:** So Tom, let's make it practical. How can someone actually take part?
**TOM:** First... next time a procedure frustrates you, write the idea down instead of just sighing.
**ANNA:** Second... visit the Zesumme Vereinfachen platform and share your suggestion, in your own language.
**TOM:** Third... if you like, follow it through one or more phases, and help co-create the solution.
**ANNA:** And fourth... remember, even a small idea can make a real difference for many people.
**TOM:** Let's bring it all together. What should people remember about Zesumme Vereinfachen?
**ANNA:** First... it is a platform where citizens and businesses help simplify the State's procedures.
**TOM:** Second... it is run by the Ministry for Digitalisation, and launched in 2022, as part of "Einfach Lëtzebuerg".
**ANNA:** Third... you can submit ideas, join one phase or many, in any of the four languages.
**TOM:** And fourth... it has already helped improve real services, like the MyGuichet app.
**ANNA:** Paperwork may never be exciting. But the chance to make it simpler, together, is genuinely empowering.
**TOM:** So the next time something feels too complicated, remember... your voice can help fix it.
**ANNA:** Thank you so much for listening today.
**TOM:** Take care, and see you in the next episode. Äddi!
**ANNA:** Äddi!

*Sources: "Zesumme Vereinfachen" platform, zesumme-vereinfachen.lu, and the Ministry for Digitalisation (mindigital.gouvernement.lu) — the participatory administrative-simplification platform managed by the Simplification Council, launched in 2022 as part of the "Einfach Lëtzebuerg" programme; citizens and businesses can submit ideas and take part in one or several project phases, in Luxembourgish, German, French or English. The example of citizen feedback on the MyGuichet.lu app is from the Ministry for Digitalisation. General information only. Accurate as of June 2026.*
