# Podcast Script — "STATEC and the Statistics Portal – Luxembourg in Numbers"

**Part of the Biergerpakt programme**
**Hosts:** Anna (woman) and Tom (man)
**Length:** about 14 minutes — spoken slowly, in simple English

---

**ANNA:** Hello, and welcome back! It's lovely to have you with us today.
**TOM:** Hello everyone!
**ANNA:** This episode is part of the Biergerpakt. That is a programme about living together in Luxembourg. The idea is simple... we want to help residents, and cross-border workers too, to understand the country.
**TOM:** And today, we are going to understand the country through numbers.
**ANNA:** Yes! How many people live here, how prices change, how the economy is doing. Facts and figures about Luxembourg.
**TOM:** And there is one official organisation whose whole job is to measure and share these numbers. It is called STATEC.
**ANNA:** STATEC is Luxembourg's national statistics institute.
**TOM:** It has a lovely motto... "We count because you count."
**ANNA:** I love that. The numbers are not just for experts. They are about people, and for people.
**TOM:** So Anna, what exactly does STATEC do?
**ANNA:** STATEC collects, produces and shares official statistics about Luxembourg... the population, the economy, prices, jobs, and much more.
**TOM:** It works under the Ministry of the Economy, but its statistical work is independent.
**ANNA:** That independence matters a lot. It means the figures are neutral and trustworthy, not bent to suit anyone.
**TOM:** So when STATEC says something, you can rely on it being measured carefully and honestly.
**ANNA:** Let's talk about the most famous number STATEC produces... inflation.
**TOM:** Inflation is how fast prices are rising. STATEC measures it every month.
**ANNA:** It does this through the consumer price index... the price of a typical basket of goods and services that households buy.
**TOM:** Food, rent, fuel, services. They track how that basket's price changes over time.
**ANNA:** And here is why it matters so much in Luxembourg. That index is linked to the famous "index" system.
**TOM:** Yes. In Luxembourg, when prices rise by a certain amount, salaries and pensions are automatically increased to keep up.
**ANNA:** That automatic adjustment is called indexation. And it is based on STATEC's measurements.
**TOM:** So STATEC's work touches almost every wallet in the country. That's remarkable.
**ANNA:** It really is. But inflation is only one of many things STATEC measures.
**TOM:** It also counts the population... how many of us there are, where we live, how the country is growing.
**ANNA:** It measures the economy, with figures like the gross domestic product, often shortened to GDP.
**TOM:** GDP is a measure of all that the country produces. STATEC tracks how the economy grows or slows.
**ANNA:** It also looks at jobs and wages, at housing, at education, at the environment, and much more.
**TOM:** Now, here's the question many people have. Where can I actually see all this?
**ANNA:** On the official statistics portal. The website is statistiques dot public dot lu.
**TOM:** And the best part... it is free, and open to everyone.
**ANNA:** You can browse tables of figures by theme, read short news releases, and explore the data yourself.
**TOM:** There are even simple tools and simulators on the portal.
**ANNA:** For example, tools about inflation and indexation, or to compare figures across the years.
**TOM:** So you don't have to be an economist. The portal is built for curious citizens too.
**ANNA:** STATEC also looks beyond money, at things that matter for our quality of life.
**TOM:** Yes. It publishes indicators on wellbeing, and on sustainable development.
**ANNA:** Because a good society is not only about how much we produce, but about how well we live.
**TOM:** That's a healthy way to look at a country... with both the head and the heart.
**ANNA:** Let's talk about why all this matters for ordinary people, even those who never open the website.
**TOM:** First, because good decisions need good facts. Governments use these numbers to plan.
**ANNA:** Schools, hospitals, housing, transport... planning all of it needs to know how many people there are, and what they need.
**TOM:** Second, because it keeps the public conversation honest.
**ANNA:** When people argue about the economy or prices, neutral figures give everyone a shared, factual starting point.
**TOM:** And third, because it is your right to know. These are public facts about your own country.
**ANNA:** Let's make this practical. How can someone actually use STATEC?
**TOM:** First, if you hear a claim about Luxembourg in the news, you can check the real figures on the portal.
**ANNA:** Second, if you're curious about prices, population or the economy, explore the tables and tools for free.
**TOM:** Third, if you're a student, a journalist, or just a curious mind, it's a goldmine of reliable information.
**ANNA:** And fourth, remember that behind the famous "index" on your payslip, there is STATEC, measuring carefully.
**TOM:** Let's bring it all together. What should people remember about STATEC?
**ANNA:** First... STATEC is Luxembourg's national statistics institute, independent and trustworthy, with the motto "we count because you count".
**TOM:** Second... it measures inflation, which feeds the indexation of salaries and pensions, plus population, the economy, and much more.
**ANNA:** Third... all of it is free to explore on the portal, statistiques dot public dot lu.
**TOM:** And fourth... it even measures wellbeing and sustainable development, not just money.
**ANNA:** Numbers can feel cold. But behind every figure is a real story about real people in this country.
**TOM:** And having honest, shared facts is one of the quiet foundations of a healthy democracy.
**ANNA:** Thank you so much for listening today.
**TOM:** Take care, and see you in the next episode. Äddi!
**ANNA:** Äddi!

*Sources: STATEC (Institut national de la statistique et des études économiques), statistiques.public.lu — the national statistics portal, the consumer price index and inflation dossier, indicators for sustainable development and GDP-wellbeing, and the portal's tables and simulators. STATEC is Luxembourg's national statistics institute, operating under the Ministry of the Economy with independence in its statistical work; the National Consumer Price Index (IPCN) is used for wage indexation. Figures such as annual inflation easing to around 2.3% in May 2026 are from STATEC and dated accordingly; figures change regularly. General information only. Accurate as of June 2026.*
