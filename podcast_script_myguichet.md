# Podcast Script — "MyGuichet.lu: Your Online Door to Luxembourg's Administration"

**Part of the Biergerpakt programme**
**Hosts:** Anna (woman) and Tom (man)
**Length:** about 10 minutes — spoken slowly, in simple English

---

**ANNA:** Hello, and welcome! It's really nice to have you with us today.

**TOM:** Hello everyone!

**ANNA:** This episode is part of the Biergerpakt. That's a programme of living together in Luxembourg. The idea is simple... we want to help residents, and also cross-border workers, to discover Luxembourg — or maybe re-discover it — and to meet new people. So you can connect, and exchange ideas.

**TOM:** Exactly. And today we talk about something very useful. Especially if you just arrived in Luxembourg. It's called MyGuichet.lu.

**ANNA:** Right. So, Tom... let's start at the beginning. What is MyGuichet.lu?

**TOM:** Okay, so... MyGuichet.lu is the official online platform of the Luxembourg government. It's secure, and it's your... let's say, your single point of contact with the public administration. So instead of going to an office, you can do many administrative things from home. On your computer, or on your phone.

**ANNA:** So it's like... an online counter?

**TOM:** Yes! Actually, that's what the word means. "Guichet" is a French word. It means "counter" — you know, the desk with the small window, where you go in an office to ask for something.

**ANNA:** Ah, okay. So MyGuichet is... "my counter". My personal counter with the State.

**TOM:** Exactly. But online. Open every day, at any hour.

**ANNA:** Nice. And, hmm... what can I actually do with it?

**TOM:** Quite a lot, really. The main thing is... you can do administrative procedures online. The website has these step-by-step forms — they call them "online assistants". They guide you through the questions, one by one. You fill them in, you add your documents, and you send everything directly to the right administration.

**ANNA:** Without printing anything?

**TOM:** For many procedures, yes. No printing, no envelope, no stamp. And there's more. On MyGuichet, you have your own personal space. They call it an "eSpace". In your eSpace, you can follow the status of your requests... so you can see, okay, my file arrived, it is being processed, and so on.

**ANNA:** That's good. Because normally you send a letter and then... silence.

**TOM:** *(laughs)* Yes, exactly. And in your eSpace you can also see the personal data the State has about you. The official data — for example from the national registry. You can also receive official messages and documents there, electronically. And... one more thing... you can book appointments online with some administrations.

**ANNA:** Okay, wait, let me repeat that. So I can... do procedures online, follow my files, check my official data, receive documents, and book appointments.

**TOM:** That's it. And you can also store important documents in your eSpace, so they are always with you. Oh — and there is a mobile app, too. MyGuichet.lu, for your smartphone.

**ANNA:** Very complete. Now... the big question. Why should I use it? I mean, I can still go to the office, no?

**TOM:** Of course, you can. But the offices are open during the day, when many of us are at work. MyGuichet is open twenty-four hours, seven days a week. You save time — no queue, no waiting. And everything stays in one place.

**ANNA:** And it's in English?

**TOM:** The website, yes — Guichet.lu exists in French, German and English. And many procedures too. Not all of them, but many. So for someone who just arrived and doesn't speak French yet... it really helps.

**ANNA:** Mmm, that's important. Okay. So now, how do I start? What do I need?

**TOM:** Right, so... to register on MyGuichet.lu, you need a few things. First, you must be at least sixteen years old. Then you need your Luxembourg national identification number. That's a thirteen-digit number — people here call it the "matricule". You get it when you register in Luxembourg, or when you start working here.

**ANNA:** The matricule, okay. Cross-border workers have one too, right?

**TOM:** Yes, if you work in Luxembourg, you have one. It's on your social security card, for example. Then you need an email address, a computer or a smartphone... and one more thing. A way to prove your identity online. A secure login, let's say.

**ANNA:** Ah, and this is where LuxTrust comes in?

**TOM:** Exactly. So... LuxTrust. Let me explain, because everyone in Luxembourg talks about LuxTrust.

**ANNA:** *(laughs)* True!

**TOM:** LuxTrust is a company that provides secure digital identity. The most popular product is LuxTrust Mobile — an app on your phone. When you log in somewhere, the app asks you to confirm. Many people already use it for their online banking here.

**ANNA:** Okay, so the question many listeners will have... do I need LuxTrust to use MyGuichet?

**TOM:** Good question. The answer is... not necessarily. You need *one* secure way to log in, but you have a choice. It can be a LuxTrust product, yes. Or the Luxembourg electronic identity card — the eID — if you have a Luxembourg ID card with the certificates activated. Or, and this is interesting for people from other European countries... an eIDAS login from your own country.

**ANNA:** eIDAS... that's the European system, right? So I could use, for example, my Belgian or German electronic ID?

**TOM:** Exactly. If your country has one of these national systems, you can often use it to log in to MyGuichet. And one more thing — some simple procedures work even *without* logging in at all. But then you have fewer options. You can't save the form and continue later, and you can't follow the status. So... having a real account is much better.

**TOM:** But honestly, if you live or work here, LuxTrust Mobile is the easy way. You install the app once, and... that's it. And if you already use it for your bank, you can use the same one.

**ANNA:** Good. So, let's say I have my matricule and my LuxTrust. What do I do?

**TOM:** It's quite simple. You go to Guichet.lu, you click on "Log in", and you follow the steps with your email and your matricule. Then you create your personal eSpace. It takes a few minutes. And there are tutorials on the website, step by step.

**ANNA:** Perfect. Now... what kind of procedures are we talking about? Which areas?

**TOM:** Almost everything in daily life, really. Let's see... citizenship — so identity card, passport, criminal record. And here is a nice one: as a foreign resident, you can apply to register on the electoral rolls — so you can vote in the communal elections, and in the European elections. Then immigration — residence permits, for example. Very important when you arrive. Then family and education... taxation — yes, you can do your tax return online. Transport — your driving licence, registering a car. Health and social security. Housing. Financial aid. Work and pension... and even leisure, like fishing permits.

**ANNA:** Wow. So really... from the passport to the fishing permit.

**TOM:** That's right. And maybe one sentence for the entrepreneurs listening: businesses can use MyGuichet too, with a business eSpace, for things like tax and VAT declarations, social security declarations for their staff, or applying for permits and licences.

**ANNA:** Good to know. Okay, Tom, let's wrap up. Short summary?

**TOM:** Sure. MyGuichet.lu is the secure online platform of the Luxembourg State. You can do your administrative procedures from home, follow your files, receive official documents, and book appointments. To use it fully, you register with your matricule and a secure login — LuxTrust, the Luxembourg eID, or a European eIDAS login from your country.

**ANNA:** And if you need help, there are tutorials on Guichet.lu, and a helpdesk — you can even book a video call. So... don't be afraid to try.

**TOM:** Really, it makes life in Luxembourg easier. Especially in the first months.

**ANNA:** That was our episode about MyGuichet.lu, part of the Biergerpakt — living together in Luxembourg, discovering the country, and meeting new people. Thank you so much for listening.

**TOM:** Thank you, everyone. Take care... and see you next time!

**ANNA:** Bye bye!
