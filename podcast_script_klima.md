# Podcast Script — "Klima-Agence – Free Advice for Your Energy and Climate Projects"

**Part of the Biergerpakt programme**
**Hosts:** Anna (woman) and Tom (man)
**Length:** about 15 minutes — spoken slowly, in simple English

---

**ANNA:** Hello, and welcome back! It's wonderful to have you with us today.
**TOM:** Hello everyone!
**ANNA:** This episode is part of the Biergerpakt. That is a programme about living together in Luxembourg. The idea is simple... we want to help residents, and cross-border workers too, to feel more at home here, and to make the most of the country.
**TOM:** And today we are looking at a topic that is on a lot of minds... energy, the climate, and our homes.
**ANNA:** Think about it. Heating bills, insulation, solar panels, electric cars, grants... it can feel like a lot to understand.
**TOM:** And it often feels expensive, and complicated. Where do you even start?
**ANNA:** Well, that is exactly why today's organisation exists. It is called Klima-Agence.
**TOM:** Klima-Agence. The name already tells us part of the story... "Klima" means climate.
**ANNA:** Yes. Klima-Agence is Luxembourg's national partner for energy and climate matters.
**TOM:** So Anna, what exactly is it? Is it a company?
**ANNA:** Not a private company in the usual sense. It is a public-interest organisation, supported by the Luxembourg government.
**TOM:** Several ministries stand behind it... the Ministry of the Economy, the Ministry of the Environment and Climate, and the Ministry of Housing.
**ANNA:** And it works together with professional bodies, like the Chamber of Skilled Trades and Crafts, and the association of architects and consulting engineers.
**TOM:** Here's a detail some people remember. Until 2022, Klima-Agence had a different name... it was called myenergy.
**ANNA:** So if you have heard of myenergy in the past, it is the same organisation, with a new name and a wider mission.
**TOM:** And what is that mission, exactly?
**ANNA:** To support the whole of society in protecting the climate and in the energy transition.
**TOM:** The energy transition... moving away from fossil fuels, towards cleaner, more efficient energy.
**ANNA:** Luxembourg has set national targets for 2030... using more renewable energy, using energy more efficiently, and cutting greenhouse gas emissions.
**TOM:** And Klima-Agence helps put those big national goals into practice, in real homes and real lives.
**ANNA:** But here is the part I really want people to hear. The advice they give to citizens is free.
**TOM:** Free, yes. And just as important... it is independent.
**ANNA:** That means they are not trying to sell you a boiler, or a particular brand of solar panel.
**TOM:** They give you neutral, expert guidance, in your interest. The service is financed by Klima-Agence itself.
**ANNA:** So you can ask your questions honestly, and get an honest answer. That is rare and valuable.
**TOM:** So how does a normal person actually get this advice?
**ANNA:** You can make an appointment with an advisor. Depending on your project, it can happen at your home, by video, or by phone.
**TOM:** Or you can simply call an advisor directly. The number is 8-0-0-2, 1-1, 9-0.
**ANNA:** And the advisors are available Monday to Friday, in the morning and in the afternoon.
**TOM:** Let's talk about what they can actually help you with. What kinds of projects?
**ANNA:** Quite a range. The first big one is insulating your home.
**TOM:** Good insulation means a warmer home in winter, a cooler home in summer, and lower heating bills.
**ANNA:** The second is building a new home in a sustainable way... a home that uses less energy from the start.
**TOM:** The third is heating with renewable energy. For example, a heat pump that draws warmth from the air or the ground.
**ANNA:** Instead of burning oil or gas, you use the sun and the earth. It's cleaner, and over time, often cheaper.
**TOM:** The fourth is generating your own clean electricity, with solar panels on your roof.
**ANNA:** Those photovoltaic panels turn sunlight into electricity, which you can use at home or feed into the grid.
**TOM:** The fifth is eco-mobility... greener ways of getting around, like electric vehicles and charging stations.
**ANNA:** And the sixth is simpler than all the others... just using less energy in your daily habits.
**TOM:** Small changes, every day, that are good for the planet and good for your wallet.
**ANNA:** So whether your project is big or small, there is guidance for it.
**TOM:** Now Anna, let's talk about money, because these projects can be expensive.
**ANNA:** They can. But the good news is that there are many grants and subsidies available.
**TOM:** And this is another place where Klima-Agence really helps. They guide you through the financial support.
**ANNA:** There are subsidies from the State, from your commune, and sometimes even from energy suppliers.
**TOM:** Keeping track of all of those would be a nightmare on your own.
**ANNA:** That's why Klima-Agence helps you understand which grants apply to your project.
**TOM:** There is even an online subsidy simulator. You enter your project, and it shows you what support you could receive.
**ANNA:** A big part of this is the national grant scheme called the Klimabonus.
**TOM:** The Klimabonus supports things like energy-efficient renovation, renewable heating, solar panels, electric vehicles, and charging stations.
**ANNA:** So it really covers most of the projects we just talked about.
**TOM:** Are there other tools on their website, beyond the subsidy simulator?
**ANNA:** Yes, some very practical ones. There is a solar simulator, to help you plan panels on your own roof.
**TOM:** And a renovation planner, to help you think through the steps of a bigger project.
**ANNA:** These tools are free to use, and they help you get a clearer picture before you spend any money.
**TOM:** Here's a question many people have. How do I find a good, trustworthy professional to do the work?
**ANNA:** Klima-Agence has a network of certified advisors and professionals you can look for.
**TOM:** So you are not just left alone to guess who is reliable.
**ANNA:** Let's talk about something I really admire about them... inclusion.
**TOM:** Yes. Their advice is meant for everyone, whatever your language, your budget, or your background.
**ANNA:** And they pay special attention to people who need the most help.
**TOM:** For example, if energy bills are becoming hard to pay, there is support available, including through social welfare offices.
**ANNA:** So this is not only for people building big new houses. It is for renters, for families on a tight budget, for everyone.
**TOM:** Now, there's an important warning we should pass on, about trust.
**ANNA:** Yes. Klima-Agence never allows private companies to use its name or its logo to sell their products.
**TOM:** So if you see an advert or a message using the Klima-Agence name to push a sale, be careful... it is not them.
**ANNA:** The real Klima-Agence gives free, neutral advice. It does not run sales campaigns through other companies.
**TOM:** If in doubt, contact Klima-Agence directly, using the official phone number or website.
**ANNA:** So Tom, let's make this really practical. How should someone actually use Klima-Agence?
**TOM:** First... before you start any energy project, get free advice. Ask your questions before you spend.
**ANNA:** Second... use the online simulators to understand the subsidies and plan your solar or renovation.
**TOM:** Third... let them help you find the right grants, like the Klimabonus, so you don't miss any support.
**ANNA:** Fourth... look for a certified professional for the actual work.
**TOM:** And fifth... remember it is free, independent, and on your side.
**ANNA:** Let's bring it all together. What should people remember about Klima-Agence?
**TOM:** First... it is Luxembourg's national agency for energy and climate, backed by the government. It used to be called myenergy.
**ANNA:** Second... it gives free, independent advice on insulation, building, heating, solar, and eco-mobility.
**TOM:** Third... it helps you find grants and subsidies, including the national Klimabonus scheme.
**ANNA:** Fourth... you can reach an advisor by phone, by video, or at home, and use free online tools.
**TOM:** And fifth... be careful of anyone misusing their name to sell you something. The real service is free.
**ANNA:** Energy and climate can feel like huge, distant topics. But they start at home, with small, doable steps.
**TOM:** And you don't have to figure it all out alone. There is free, friendly, expert help waiting for you.
**ANNA:** Thank you so much for listening today.
**TOM:** Take care, and see you in the next episode. Äddi!
**ANNA:** Äddi!
