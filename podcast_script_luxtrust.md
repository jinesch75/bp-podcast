# Podcast Script — "LuxTrust: Your Digital Identity in Luxembourg"

**Part of the Biergerpakt programme**
**Hosts:** Anna (woman) and Tom (man)
**Length:** about 11 minutes — spoken slowly, in simple English

---

**ANNA:** Hello, and welcome! It's really nice to have you with us today.

**TOM:** Hello everyone!

**ANNA:** This episode is part of the Biergerpakt. That's a programme of living together in Luxembourg. The idea is simple... we want to help residents, and also cross-border workers, to discover Luxembourg — or re-discover it — and to meet new people.

**TOM:** Exactly. And today we talk about something you will need almost everywhere in Luxembourg, online. It's called LuxTrust.

**ANNA:** LuxTrust. We have mentioned it before, in the episode about MyGuichet. But today, the full story. Tom, let's start simply. What is LuxTrust?

**TOM:** So, LuxTrust gives you one thing — a digital identity. A single, secure digital identity that lets you prove who you really are, online.

**ANNA:** A digital identity. Can you explain that a bit more?

**TOM:** Of course. Think of it as the digital version of your ID card or your passport. In real life, you show your ID card to prove who you are. But online, nobody can see you. So you need something that proves your identity in a secure way. That's the digital identity.

**ANNA:** Ah. So it's like an ID card, but for the internet.

**TOM:** Exactly. And LuxTrust is the company that provides it. They are what we call a "Certificate Authority" and a "trust service provider". They are certified by the European Union, and they have been doing this for about twenty years.

**ANNA:** Twenty years. So it's well established. And is my data safe with them?

**TOM:** Yes. Your data is encrypted, and stored in data centres here, in Luxembourg. Security is really the whole point of the company.

**ANNA:** Good. Now the most important question for most people. What can I actually do with my LuxTrust identity?

**TOM:** A lot of everyday things. Let me give the four main uses. The first, and the most common — your bank. With LuxTrust, you log in to your online banking securely, and you confirm your transactions. A payment, a transfer... you validate it with LuxTrust.

**ANNA:** So most people in Luxembourg first meet LuxTrust through their bank.

**TOM:** Exactly. The second use — public services. With LuxTrust, you log in to MyGuichet.lu. So you can do your administrative procedures, and, for example, complete and sign your tax declaration online.

**ANNA:** That's the link with our MyGuichet episode. Okay, the third?

**TOM:** The third is a beautiful one — the electronic signature. With LuxTrust, you can sign a document electronically. And — this is the key point — that electronic signature has the same legal value as a handwritten signature.

**ANNA:** Wait, really? The same legal value as signing with a pen?

**TOM:** The same. So you can close a contract, or sign an official document, from your home, without printing anything.

**ANNA:** That's powerful. And the fourth use?

**TOM:** The fourth — your online purchases. When you shop online, LuxTrust can be used, together with the system called 3D Secure, to confirm that it's really you paying. So your card payments are more secure.

**ANNA:** So, to sum up — banking, public services, electronic signature, and online payments.

**TOM:** That's it. One identity, for all of these.

**ANNA:** And quickly — is it only for individuals like us? Or also for companies?

**TOM:** Mostly we talk about individuals today. But yes, businesses use LuxTrust too — to identify their customers, to sign and seal documents, and to secure their processes. But for you, as a resident or cross-border worker, the personal uses are what matter most.

**ANNA:** Perfect. Now, the practical part. How does it actually work, when I use it?

**TOM:** Let's take the example of logging in to your bank. Step one — you connect to your bank's website or app. Step two — you choose your LuxTrust device. Step three — you enter your User ID and your password. Or, if you have a card-based device, your PIN code. And step four — you generate a one-time password, an OTP — a code that works only once — and that gives you access.

**ANNA:** A one-time password. So a new code each time.

**TOM:** Each time. That's what makes it secure. With the LuxTrust app, this all happens in a few taps on your phone.

**ANNA:** Speaking of which — what are the different LuxTrust products? Because I think there's a choice.

**TOM:** There is. For an individual, there are three main products. The first, and today the most popular — the LuxTrust Mobile app. It's a one-hundred-percent mobile solution. Everything happens on your smartphone.

**ANNA:** So no extra device to carry. Just my phone.

**TOM:** Just your phone. The second product is the Scan — a small physical device, with a little screen and a camera, that generates your codes. And the third is the SmartCard — a chip card, like a bank card. But to use the SmartCard, you need a card reader and some software on your computer.

**ANNA:** And which one should most people choose?

**TOM:** For most people today, the app is the easiest. And here's an important note — the old LuxTrust Token, the little device many people had on their keychain, was stopped at the end of 2024. So if you still have an old Token, it's time to switch to the app.

**ANNA:** Good to know. And — roughly — what does it cost?

**TOM:** It's a paid service, valid for three years. As a rough idea, the app starts at around fifty euros for three years, and the physical devices cost a bit more. But please check the exact prices on luxtrust.com, because they can change.

**ANNA:** Always check the website for the exact price. Okay, Tom — now the big practical question. I'm new here, I have nothing yet. How do I get a LuxTrust identity? Step by step.

**TOM:** Five steps. Step one — choose the right product for you, for example the app. Step two — place your order. You can order online, on the LuxTrust website, or through your bank.

**ANNA:** Ah, so my bank can set it up for me.

**TOM:** Very often, yes — many people get their LuxTrust through their bank. Step three, and this one is important — you must get identified. Because they are creating proof of your identity, they must check who you are. You can do this face-to-face, with one of their agents. Or remotely, by a video identification.

**ANNA:** So, a bit like opening a bank account. They verify it's really me.

**TOM:** Exactly. Step four — you receive your LuxTrust codes, and your device if you ordered one. And step five — you activate your identity. After that, your digital identity is valid for three years.

**ANNA:** Let's stay on that last step — activation. What do I need to have ready?

**TOM:** Good question. Before you start, have these things at hand. Your User ID — you receive it by letter, or by SMS. Your initial password — also by letter or SMS. Your device, if you have one. And your activation code.

**ANNA:** The activation code — where does that come from?

**TOM:** You actually chose it yourself, when you ordered. They usually advise you to use the last five characters of your ID card or passport number. And you also find it in the order confirmation, sent to you by email.

**ANNA:** Okay. And how does the activation itself go?

**TOM:** It depends on the product. For the Mobile app, you activate it directly on your smartphone, following the guide. For the Scan, it takes about six minutes — you follow the steps, scan some QR codes, and set a new, strong password. For the SmartCard, it's a bit longer, around twenty minutes, because you first install the software, called the middleware, on your computer.

**ANNA:** And once I finish activating — can I use it immediately?

**TOM:** Almost. One small thing to remember — after activation, the certificate becomes usable after about four hours. So don't activate it five minutes before you need it. Do it a little in advance.

**ANNA:** Very practical tip. Activate it the evening before, not at the last minute.

**TOM:** Exactly.

**ANNA:** Now, Tom — this is digital identity, it's sensitive. Let's talk about safety. About scams.

**TOM:** Yes, this is really important. There are phishing attempts — fake messages, fake phone calls, that pretend to be LuxTrust. So remember a few rules. LuxTrust will never ask you for your codes or your credentials. Never.

**ANNA:** Never give the codes. Even if it looks official?

**TOM:** Even then. And more — LuxTrust will never call you to ask for sensitive information. They will never ask for access to your computer or your phone. And they will never come to your home.

**ANNA:** So if someone does any of that, it's a scam.

**TOM:** It's a scam. Your digital identity is yours, and only yours. Just like you don't give your bank card and your PIN to a colleague, you never share your LuxTrust credentials. If you are in doubt, contact LuxTrust directly, with the number on their official website.

**ANNA:** That's the golden rule again — when in doubt, don't share, and check directly. Okay, Tom, let's make it really practical for someone listening. What should I do?

**TOM:** If you live or work in Luxembourg and you don't have it yet — get a LuxTrust identity. The easiest way: ask your bank, or go to luxtrust.com, choose the app, order it, get identified, and activate it. Then you can use it for your bank, for MyGuichet, for signing documents, and for your online payments.

**ANNA:** One identity, for your whole digital life here. Short summary to finish?

**TOM:** Sure. LuxTrust is your secure digital identity in Luxembourg — like an ID card for the internet, from a European-certified provider. You use it for online banking, for public services like MyGuichet, for legally-valid electronic signatures, and for online payments. You get it through your bank or on luxtrust.com, you prove your identity in person or by video, and you activate it — most easily with the Mobile app.

**ANNA:** And the safety message?

**TOM:** LuxTrust never asks for your codes, never calls for sensitive data, never asks to access your devices. Keep your credentials to yourself. When in doubt, check the official website.

**ANNA:** Beautiful. So... your digital identity is the key to daily life online in Luxembourg. Set it up once, and so much becomes easier.

**TOM:** Really. A little effort to set it up... and then everything, from your sofa.

**ANNA:** That was our episode about LuxTrust, part of the Biergerpakt — living together in Luxembourg, discovering the country, and meeting new people. Thank you so much for listening.

**TOM:** Thank you, everyone. Take care... and see you next time!

**ANNA:** Bye bye!
