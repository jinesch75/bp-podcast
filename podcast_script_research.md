# Podcast Script — "Research Luxembourg – The Country's Public Research Institutions"

**Part of the Biergerpakt programme**
**Hosts:** Anna (woman) and Tom (man)
**Length:** about 14 minutes — spoken slowly, in simple English

---

**ANNA:** Hello, and welcome back! It's wonderful to have you with us today.
**TOM:** Hello everyone!
**ANNA:** This episode is part of the Biergerpakt. That is a programme about living together in Luxembourg. The idea is simple... we want to help residents, and cross-border workers too, to discover the country.
**TOM:** And today we're discovering a side of Luxembourg that many people don't know about... research and science.
**ANNA:** When we think of Luxembourg, we often think of banks, or of being small. But there's a growing world of science here too.
**TOM:** New medicines, clean technologies, studies of society, the digital future... all being explored by researchers in Luxembourg.
**ANNA:** And there's a shared name that brings these efforts together. It is called Research Luxembourg.
**TOM:** So Anna, what is Research Luxembourg, exactly?
**ANNA:** It is a joint initiative of the country's main public research players, supported by the Ministry of Higher Education and Research.
**TOM:** So instead of each institution working alone, they also act together, under one banner, to promote research in Luxembourg.
**ANNA:** Its spirit is to learn, to explore, and to make a real impact for a better future.
**TOM:** Let's meet the main players, because each one has its own role.
**ANNA:** The first is the University of Luxembourg. It is the country's public university.
**TOM:** It's quite young... it was created in 2003. But it has grown fast, and is very international.
**ANNA:** It is where students study and where a great deal of research happens, across many fields.
**TOM:** The second is the Luxembourg Institute of Science and Technology, often called LIST.
**ANNA:** LIST works on applied science and technology... materials, the environment, and digital tools that industry can use.
**TOM:** The third is the Luxembourg Institute of Health, or LIH.
**ANNA:** As the name says, it focuses on health... medical research to better understand, prevent and treat disease.
**TOM:** The fourth is the Luxembourg Institute of Socio-Economic Research, or LISER.
**ANNA:** We've met LISER before, in our episode on the Observatoire de l'Habitat. It studies society and the economy.
**TOM:** Things like work, housing, education, inequality... research that helps us understand how people actually live.
**ANNA:** The fifth player is a little different. It is the National Research Fund, often called the FNR.
**TOM:** The FNR doesn't mainly do research itself. It funds research, and supports researchers across the country.
**ANNA:** So it's a bit like the engine room... helping good ideas get the resources they need.
**TOM:** And the sixth is Luxinnovation, the national innovation agency.
**ANNA:** Luxinnovation helps connect research with businesses, so that good ideas become real products and jobs.
**TOM:** So together you have universities, institutes, a funder, and an innovation agency, all pulling in the same direction.
**ANNA:** There's also a special place at the heart of all this... Belval.
**TOM:** Belval is a former steelworks site in the south of the country, in Esch-sur-Alzette.
**ANNA:** Where furnaces once made steel, there is now a modern campus for research and learning.
**TOM:** It's a beautiful symbol... a country moving from heavy industry towards knowledge and innovation.
**ANNA:** Let's talk about why this matters, even for people who will never work in a laboratory.
**TOM:** First, because research improves daily life. Better health, cleaner energy, smarter services.
**ANNA:** Second, because it builds the future economy. Good research attracts talent, companies, and good jobs.
**TOM:** Third, because it helps us understand ourselves... our society, our challenges, and how to face them.
**ANNA:** And fourth, because it's a source of pride. A small country can do world-class science.
**TOM:** There's also something here for young people, and for newcomers.
**ANNA:** Yes. Research Luxembourg supports research careers, and helps people start a PhD or find a science job.
**TOM:** So if you, or your children, are drawn to science, this whole world is open and growing.
**ANNA:** And it's deeply international. Researchers come from all over the world to work here, side by side.
**TOM:** Which fits Luxembourg perfectly... many languages, many backgrounds, one shared curiosity.
**ANNA:** Let's make it practical. What can someone take from this?
**TOM:** First, know that Luxembourg has real, serious public research... in health, science, technology and society.
**ANNA:** Second, if you're curious, you can follow Research Luxembourg's news and discoveries.
**TOM:** Third, if you or someone you know loves science, there are studies, PhDs and careers to explore here.
**ANNA:** And fourth, remember that this research quietly shapes better medicines, greener tech, and wiser policies.
**TOM:** Let's bring it all together. What should people remember?
**ANNA:** First... Research Luxembourg unites the country's main public research players under one banner.
**TOM:** Second... they include the University of Luxembourg, the institutes LIST, LIH and LISER, the funder FNR, and Luxinnovation.
**ANNA:** Third... much of it is gathered at Belval, a former steel site reborn as a campus of knowledge.
**TOM:** And fourth... this research improves health, the economy, and our understanding of society, and it welcomes new talent.
**ANNA:** A country is not only its past. It is also what it chooses to discover, and to build.
**TOM:** And Luxembourg is choosing, more and more, to build with knowledge.
**ANNA:** Thank you so much for listening today.
**TOM:** Take care, stay curious, and see you in the next episode. Äddi!
**ANNA:** Äddi!
