# Podcast Script — "Lifelong Learning – It's Never Too Late to Learn"

**Part of the Biergerpakt programme**
**Hosts:** Anna (woman) and Tom (man)
**Length:** about 10 minutes — spoken slowly, in simple English

---

**ANNA:** Hello, and welcome back! It's lovely to have you with us today.
**TOM:** Hello everyone!
**ANNA:** This episode is part of the Biergerpakt. That is a programme about living together in Luxembourg. The idea is simple... we want to help residents, and cross-border workers too, to grow and to find their place.
**TOM:** And today's topic is full of hope... the idea that we never stop learning.
**ANNA:** School is just the beginning. Learning can continue through our whole lives.
**TOM:** A new job, a promotion, a change of career, a new language, or simply a passion to follow.
**ANNA:** Maybe you missed a diploma when you were younger. Maybe your industry is changing. Maybe you just moved here.
**TOM:** Whatever your story, there is a way forward. And in Luxembourg, there's one place that brings it all together.
**ANNA:** It's the lifelong learning portal. The website is lifelong dash learning dot lu.
**TOM:** Its motto says it beautifully... "develop your skills throughout life".
**ANNA:** So Tom, what exactly is this portal?
**TOM:** It's the national portal for continuing training, run by the institute for continuing vocational training, the INFPC.
**ANNA:** And its heart is an enormous catalogue of training courses.
**TOM:** We're talking about more than thirteen thousand courses, from hundreds of approved training providers.
**ANNA:** All gathered in one place, so you don't have to search across the whole internet.
**TOM:** And because the providers are approved, you know the offer is serious and reliable.
**ANNA:** You can also search it in clever ways... by subject, or by the job you'd like to do.
**TOM:** So you can start from what interests you, or from where you want your career to go.
**ANNA:** What kinds of subjects are we talking about?
**TOM:** All sorts! Computers and technology, languages, business and human resources, finance and law.
**ANNA:** And personal development, quality and safety, project management, and so much more.
**TOM:** Whatever you want to learn, there's a good chance you'll find a course for it.
**ANNA:** The portal is built for three groups of people. Let's go through them.
**TOM:** First, individuals... people like you and me, looking to learn or to grow.
**ANNA:** Second, companies, who want to train their teams and keep their skills up to date.
**TOM:** And third, training professionals, the people and organisations who actually run the courses.
**ANNA:** Let's focus on individuals first, because there's something powerful here.
**TOM:** As an adult, you can do far more than just take a short course. You can earn real, recognised diplomas.
**ANNA:** Yes. Through continuing training, adults can gain proper qualifications, step by step.
**TOM:** There's even adult apprenticeship, where you learn a trade and earn a diploma later in life.
**ANNA:** And there are routes to secondary school diplomas, technical certificates, and higher qualifications.
**TOM:** There's also the master craftsman's diploma, which lets skilled workers run their own business and train apprentices.
**ANNA:** So if life meant you missed a qualification earlier, there's a real second chance to earn it now.
**TOM:** And that matters, because a diploma can open doors that stay closed without one.
**ANNA:** Now here's something I find truly wonderful... the validation of experience.
**TOM:** This is for people who've learned a great deal through their work and their life, but have no diploma to show it.
**ANNA:** Think of someone who has done a job expertly for fifteen years, but never had the paper to prove it.
**TOM:** Through this process, that real experience can be assessed and turned into an official diploma.
**ANNA:** So years of skill and hard work can finally be recognised, formally, on paper.
**TOM:** It's a way of honouring what people already know, not just what they've sat exams for. That's powerful.
**ANNA:** Now, learning costs time, and sometimes money. So let's talk about the help available.
**TOM:** For individuals, there are special kinds of paid leave, just for following training.
**ANNA:** The main one is individual training leave, which lets you take paid days off work to learn.
**TOM:** There's also a special leave to learn a language, which is wonderful in a country with several.
**ANNA:** So you can take time to study, without losing all of your income while you do it.
**TOM:** There's more, too. Some training costs can be deducted from your taxes.
**ANNA:** There's support for jobseekers who need training to get back into work.
**TOM:** And there are reduction vouchers that lower the price of certain courses.
**ANNA:** So cost and time, the two big barriers, are made smaller by real, practical help.
**TOM:** And it's not only individuals who get support. Companies do too.
**ANNA:** Yes. The State helps co-finance the training that companies give their staff.
**TOM:** So businesses are encouraged to invest in their people, and the whole country gets more skilled.
**ANNA:** It's a shared effort... the worker, the company, and the State, all investing in skills together.
**TOM:** Let's talk about why this matters so much, right now.
**ANNA:** The world is changing fast... new technologies, the green transition, new ways of working.
**TOM:** Jobs are changing, and some tasks that people do today will be done differently tomorrow.
**ANNA:** The portal even highlights the skills of the future... digital skills, green skills, and soft skills.
**TOM:** Digital skills help us work with new tools. Green skills help us work more sustainably.
**ANNA:** And soft skills means things like communication, teamwork and adaptability... human skills that never go out of date.
**TOM:** Keeping our skills fresh is how we stay confident, employable, and open to new chances.
**ANNA:** And it's not only about jobs. There's real joy in learning something new at any age.
**TOM:** Learning a language to talk with new neighbours. Picking up a craft. Understanding the world a little better.
**ANNA:** Now, some people feel a bit lost, and don't know where to start. The portal helps with that too.
**TOM:** There's a service called a skills audit, to help you understand your strengths and what suits you.
**ANNA:** It's like taking stock of everything you can do, and using that to choose a clear next step.
**TOM:** And there's guidance and support for people changing direction, or returning to work after a break.
**ANNA:** There's even a national school for adults, for those who want to go back and study.
**TOM:** And second-chance routes, for people who left school early and want to pick up where they stopped.
**ANNA:** So whatever your starting point, there's a path, and someone to help you find it.
**TOM:** The portal also connects to useful European tools, which are worth knowing about.
**ANNA:** For example, Europass, a kind of European skills passport, to present your skills clearly across Europe.
**TOM:** And common frameworks that make your language level, or your qualification, understandable in other countries.
**ANNA:** That's really valuable in a country as international as Luxembourg, where careers often cross borders.
**TOM:** There's also a Training Observatory, which studies how training and the job market are changing.
**ANNA:** So the system keeps learning about itself, and improving, based on real evidence.
**TOM:** And if you simply want to stay informed, there's a newsletter about training news and opportunities.
**ANNA:** Let's gently say something encouraging here, because this topic can stir up old doubts.
**TOM:** Many adults carry a quiet voice that says, "I'm too old to learn", or "I was never good at school".
**ANNA:** But learning as an adult is different. You bring experience, motivation, and a real reason to be there.
**TOM:** And you don't have to do it all at once. One course, one step, one small skill at a time.
**ANNA:** Every expert was once a beginner. And every new skill is a door you open for your future self.
**TOM:** So let's make this really practical. What can people do with all of this?
**ANNA:** First, browse the catalogue at lifelong dash learning dot lu... search by subject, or by the job you want.
**TOM:** Second, if you have experience but no diploma, look into validating that experience.
**ANNA:** Third, before you enrol, check the financial help, like training leave, tax deductions and vouchers.
**TOM:** And fourth, if you feel unsure where to begin, ask for a skills audit, or for guidance.
**ANNA:** Let's bring it all together. What should people remember?
**TOM:** First... lifelong dash learning dot lu is Luxembourg's national portal for continuing training, run by the INFPC.
**ANNA:** Second... it offers a huge catalogue of courses, and even routes to real, recognised diplomas as an adult.
**TOM:** Third... you can validate your life experience into a diploma, and get financial help, including paid training leave.
**ANNA:** And fourth... it offers guidance, a skills audit, and second chances, so anyone can find their next step.
**TOM:** Learning is one of the great joys, and one of the great equalisers, of life.
**ANNA:** It can lift a career, rebuild confidence, and open a whole new chapter, at any age.
**TOM:** And in Luxembourg, the door to it is open, for everyone, whenever you're ready to walk through.
**ANNA:** So if there's something you've always wanted to learn... maybe today is the day to begin.
**TOM:** Thank you so much for listening today.
**ANNA:** Take care, keep learning, and see you in the next episode. Äddi!
**TOM:** Äddi!

*Sources: lifelong-learning.lu, the national lifelong-learning portal powered by the INFPC (Institut National pour le développement de la Formation Professionnelle Continue) — a searchable catalogue of continuing-training courses from approved providers (search by domain or by profession); diplomas accessible through continuing training (including adult apprenticeship, secondary diplomas, BTS and the brevet de maîtrise); the validation of acquired experience (VAE); financial support for individuals (special paid leave such as individual training leave and language leave, tax deductibility, support for jobseekers, reduction vouchers) and co-funding of company training; a focus on digital, green and soft skills; guidance and retraining services (including a skills audit / bilan de compétences, the National School for Adults and second-chance routes); European tools such as Europass; and the Training Observatory. General information only — eligibility and figures change; confirm current details on the portal. Accurate as of June 2026.*
